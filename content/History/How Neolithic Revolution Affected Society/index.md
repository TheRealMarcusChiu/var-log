# Hunter Gatherer
- hunter-gatherers have few material possessions but much leisure time. They have a richer social ife than the "affluent" of the industrialized North
- hunter-gatherer groups tended to range in size from an extended family to a larger band of no more than about 100 people

# Neolithic Revolution
- increased food production lead to surplus
- the establishment of permanent settlements
- surplus led to storage and trade-routes with external societies
<br>
- the domestication of plants and animals
- having leisure time to learn, explore, and invent
- the rise of technologically sophisticated societies
- the advancement of tools for farming, war, and art
<br>
- eating food grown on farms
- consume domesticated plants and animal meat
<br>
- the narrowing  of diversity of foods available
- surplus lead to decline in nutrition (e.g. maize is deficient in some amino acids)
<br>
- the rise of infections diseases contracted from:
	- domesticated animals
	- human waste
	- rodents and parasites attracted to food storages
<br>
- living in homes, close to other people in towns and cities

<br>

- protected by laws
- increased ownership of property
- the rise of societal inequality
