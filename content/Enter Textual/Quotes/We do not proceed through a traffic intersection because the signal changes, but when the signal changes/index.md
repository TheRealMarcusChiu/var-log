# Semantics

The phrase "we do not proceed through a traffic intersection because the signal changes, but when the signal changes" seems to express a distinction or clarification about the behavior of proceeding through a traffic intersection.

The meaning of this statement can be interpreted as follows:
1. "We do not proceed through a traffic intersection because the signal changes": This suggests that the action of proceeding through an intersection is not solely dependent on the fact that the traffic signal changes from red to green. In other words, the signal changing alone is not a sufficient condition for proceeding.
2. "But when the signal changes": This part of the statement implies that there are additional factors or conditions that need to be considered before proceeding through the intersection. These factors could include ensuring that the intersection is clear of any oncoming traffic or pedestrians, and it is safe to proceed.

Overall, this statement emphasizes the importance of not blindly following the signal change but rather considering other relevant factors and exercising caution before proceeding through a traffic intersection. It highlights the need for responsible and attentive driving behavior.
# Difference Between When and Because

The words "when" and "because" are both conjunctions used to connect ideas and clauses in a sentence, but they serve different purposes and convey different meanings.
1. "When": "When" is used to indicate a specific time or condition at which something happens or will happen. It is often used to express a temporal relationship between two events. For example:
	- "I will call you when I get home." (referring to a specific time)
	- "He gets nervous when he's in crowded places." (referring to a condition or circumstance)
2. "Because": "Because" is used to provide a reason or explanation for something. It indicates cause-and-effect or the motivation behind an action. For example:
	- "She couldn't attend the meeting because she was sick." (providing a reason for not attending)
	- "The flight was canceled because of bad weather." (explaining the cause of the cancellation)

In summary, "when" is used to denote a specific time or condition, while "because" is used to provide a reason or explanation. "When" refers to a point in time or a circumstance, whereas "because" provides the cause or motive behind an event or action.
