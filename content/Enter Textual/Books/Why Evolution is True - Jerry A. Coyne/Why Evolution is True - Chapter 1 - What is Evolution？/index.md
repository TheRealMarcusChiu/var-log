# What is Evolution?

Life on Earth evolved gradually beginning with one primitive species - perhaps a self-replicating molecule - that lived more than 3.5 billion years ago; it then branched out over time, throwing off many new and diverse species; and the mechanism for most (but not all) of evolution change is natural selection

This statement can be broken down into 6 components:

> [!expand-ui]- Evolution
>
> - evolution simply means that a species undergoes genetic change over time

> [!expand-ui]- Gradualism
>
> - gradualism states that it takes many generations to produce a substantial evolutionary change

- although all species evolve, they don't do so at the same rate. Some like horseshoe crabs, coelacanth "living fossil", and gingko trees, have barely changed over millions of years
- each species do not evolve at an even pace; Just as different species vary in how fast they evolve, so a single species evolves faster or slower as evolutionary pressures wax and wane. When natural selection is strong, as when an animal or plant colonizes a new environment, evolutionary change can be fast. Once a species becomes well adapted to a stable habitat, evolution often slows down.

> [!expand-ui]- Speciation
>
> - speciation simply means the evolution of different groups that can't interbreed (i.e. groups that can't exchange genes)
> - there are many ways this can happen:
>   - members of different animal species may no longer find each other attractive as mates
>   - offspring could be sterile
>   - different plant species can use different pollinators or flower at different time, preventing cross-fertilization
> - speciation doesn't happen often, but each time one species splits into 2, it doubles the number of opportunities for future speciation

> [!expand-ui]- Common Ancestry
>
> - common ancestry simply means that we can always look back in time, using either DNA sequences or fossils, and find descendant lineages fusing at their ancestors

> [!expand-ui]- Natural Selection
>
> - natural selection explains apparent design in nature by a purely materialistic
>   process that doesn’t require creation or guidance by supernatural forces
> - the idea of natural selection:
>   - If individuals within a species differ genetically from one another, and some of those differences affect an individual’s ability to survive and reproduce in its environment, then in the next generation the “good” genes that lead to higher survival and reproduction will have relatively more copies than the “not so good” genes. Over time, the population will gradually become more and more suited to its environment as helpful mutations arise and spread through the population, while deleterious ones are weeded out. Ultimately, this process produces organisms that are well adapted to their habitats and way of life.

# Evolution is Just a Theory?

Ronald Reagan addressed an evangelical group in TX in 1980, "Well, it is a theory. It is a scientific theory only, and it has in recent years been challenged in the world of science and is not yet believed in the scientific community to be as infallible as it once was believed"

The implication is that a theory is just a mere guess, as in "My theory is that Fred is crazy about Sue." But in science the word "theory" is the highest compliment to an idea.

- Theory is much more than just a speculation about how things are: it is a well thought out group of propositions meant to explain facts about the real world. "Atomic theory" isn't just the statement that "atoms exist": it's a statement about how atoms interact with one another, form compounds, and behave chemically.
- for a theory to be considered scientific, it must be <strong>testable</strong> and make <strong>verifiable predictions</strong>.

Here are some evolutionary predictions:

- deepest/oldest layers of rocks would contains fossils of more primitive species
- there should be some cases of speciation in the fossil record
- there should be some examples of species that link together major groups suspected to have a common ancestry (e.g. birds with reptiles, and fish with amphibians)
- imperfection is the mark of evolution; not conscious design
- we should be able to see natural selection acting in the wild

Here are some evolutionary "retrodictions" (i.e. facts and data that aren't necessarily predicted by the theory of evolution, but makes sense only in light of the theory of evolution)

- patterns of species distribution on the Earth's surface
- peculiarities of how organisms develop from embryos
- existence of vestigial features that are of no apparent use
