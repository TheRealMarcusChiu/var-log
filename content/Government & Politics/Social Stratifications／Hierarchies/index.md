# Terminology
- <strong>overlord</strong> - a ruler, especially a feudal lord
- <strong>emperor</strong> - a sovereign ruler, especially over an empire
- <strong>king</strong> - a male ruler of an independent state
- <strong>monarch</strong> - a sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor
- <strong>Sultan</strong> - Originally an Arabic abstract noun meaning rulership or leader, it later came to be used as title for certain rulers who claimed almost full sovereignty in practical terms (lack of dependence on any higher ruler) but without claiming overall caliphate, or to refer to a powerful governor of a province within the caliphate. It isn’t an inherited title.
- <strong>Shah</strong> - This is the Farsi title given to the emperors, kings, princes and lords of Persia now called Iran.
- <strong>Sheikh</strong> - is an honorific Arabic language title commonly used to designate ruler of a tribe who inherited the title from his father. The title of Sheikh is given to a royal male at birth and title Sheikha a royal female at birth.
- <strong>pharaoh</strong> - a common title of Egyptian monarchs(originally meaning “great house” or “palace”)
<br>
- <strong>vizier</strong> - a high official in some Muslim countries, especially in Turkey under Ottoman rule
- <strong>noble</strong> - belonging to a hereditary class with high social or political status; aristocratic

- <strong>peasant - </strong>a poor farmer of low social status who owns or rents a small piece of land for cultivation (chiefly in historical use or with reference to subsistence farming in poorer countries)
- <strong>serf</strong> - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system to work on his lord's estate
- <strong>slave</strong> - a person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them
- <strong>servant</strong> - a person who performs duties for others, especially a person employed in a house on domestic duties or as a personal attendant
- <strong>bondservant</strong> - a person bound in service without wages
