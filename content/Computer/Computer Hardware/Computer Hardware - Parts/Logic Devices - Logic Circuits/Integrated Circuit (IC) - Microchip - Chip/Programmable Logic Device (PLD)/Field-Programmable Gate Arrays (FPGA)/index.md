###### Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGA)

- are [[Integrated Circuit (IC) - Microchip - Chip|integrated circuits (ICs)]] that fall under the umbrella of [[Programmable Logic Device (PLD)|programmable logic devices (PLDs)]]
- are equipped with [[Configurable Logic Block (CLB) - Logic Array Block (LAB)|configurable logic blocks (CLBs)]] and other features that can be programmed and reprogrammed by a user
- the term “field-programmable” indicates that the FPGA’s abilities are adjustable and not hardwired by the manufacturer
- the first commercial-use FPGA was invented in 1985 by Xilinx
