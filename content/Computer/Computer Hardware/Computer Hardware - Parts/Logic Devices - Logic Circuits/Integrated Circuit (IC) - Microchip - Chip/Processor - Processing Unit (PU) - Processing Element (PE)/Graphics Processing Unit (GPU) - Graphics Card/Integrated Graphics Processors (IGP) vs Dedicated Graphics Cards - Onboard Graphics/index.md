###### Integrated Graphics Processors (IGP) vs Dedicated Graphics Cards

```excerpt
- both are types of [[Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) - Graphics Card|GPUs]]
- <strong>Integrated Graphics Processor</strong> is a GPU built within a [[Central Processing Unit (CPU) - Central／Main Processor - Microprocessor (uP) - Microcontroller (uC) - Microcontroller Unit (MCU)|CPU]]
- <strong>Dedicated Graphics Card</strong> is a GPU as a standalone card
	- <strong>Onboard Graphics</strong> is a GPU integrated onto a [[Motherboard (MoBo)|motherboard]]
```

^excerpt

# Integrated Graphics Processors (IGP)

In personal computers, the IGPs are mostly manufactured by Intel and AMD and are integrated onto their CPUs. They are commonly known as:

- [[Intel® Processors／CPUs - Intel Graphics Technology|Intel HD and Iris Graphics]] - also called <em>HD series</em> and <em>Iris series</em>
- [[AMD Processors／CPUs|AMD Accelerated Processing Unit (APU)]] - also formerly known as: <em>fusion</em>

# Dedicated Graphics Cards

The most popular dedicated graphics cards are manufactured by [[Advanced Micro Devices (AMD)|AMD]] and [Nvidia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nvidia). They are commonly known as:

- [[AMD Radeon]]
- [[Nvidia GeForce]]
