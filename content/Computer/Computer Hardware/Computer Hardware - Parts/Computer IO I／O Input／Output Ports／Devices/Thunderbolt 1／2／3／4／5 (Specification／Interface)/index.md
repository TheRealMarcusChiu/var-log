###### Thunderbolt 3/4/5

```excerpt
- developed by [[Intel Corporation|Intel]] and Apple
- combines [[Peripheral Component Interconnect Express (PCIe - PCI-e) Specification／Interface|PCIe]] and [[High Definition Multimedia Interface (HDMI) - DisplayPort (DP)|DisplayPort]] into two serial signals and DC power via a single cable
- up to 6 peripherals may be supported through one connector
- Thunderbolt 1/2 uses the Mini DisplayPort (MDP)
- Thunderbolt 3/4/5 uses the USB-C connector
```

^excerpt

# Thunderbolt - Bitrate

- Thunderbolt 1: 2 channels, 10 Gbit/s each (20 Gbit/s in total)
- Thunderbolt 2: 20 Gbit/s in total
- Thunderbolt 3: 40 Gbit/s bidirectional
- Thunderbolt 4: 40 Gbit/s bidirectional
- Thunderbolt 5: 80 Gbit/s bidirectional

# Thunderbolt - Protocol

- Thunderbolt 1: x4 [[Peripheral Component Interconnect Express (PCIe - PCI-e) Specification／Interface|PCI Express]] 2.0, [[High Definition Multimedia Interface (HDMI) - DisplayPort (DP)|DisplayPort]] 1.1a
- Thunderbolt 2: x4 PCI Express 2.0, DisplayPort 1.2
- Thunderbolt 3: x4 PCI Express 3.0, DisplayPort 1.2, [[Universal Serial Bus (USB) 2.0／3.0／3.2／4.0 Gen 1／2 (Specification／Interface)|USB]] 3.1 Gen 2
- Thunderbolt 4: x4 PCI Express 3.0, DisplayPort 2.0, USB 4
- Thunderbolt 5: x4 PCI Express 4.0, DisplayPort 2.1, USB 4
