The <strong>Digital Signature Standard (DSS)</strong> is a [Federal Information Processing Standard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Information_Processing_Standards) specifying a suite of algorithms that can be used to generate [[Digital Signatures|digital signatures]] established by the U.S. [National Institute of Standards and Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Institute_of_Standards_and_Technology) (NIST) in 1994.

Four revisions to the initial specification have been released:

- FIPS 186-1 in 1996
- FIPS 186-2 in 2000
- FIPS 186-3 in 2009
- FIPS 186-4 in 2013
