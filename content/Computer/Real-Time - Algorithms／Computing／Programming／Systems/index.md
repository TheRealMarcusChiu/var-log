<font style="color: rgb(128,128,128);"><em>"right move at the right time"</em></font>

In computer science, <strong>real-time computing</strong>, or reactive computing describes hardware and software systems subject to a "real-time constraint", for example from event to system response. Real-time programs must guarantee a response within specified time constraints, often referred to as "deadlines"

real-time systems have a dual notion of correctness:

- logical correctness - do the right thing
- temporal correctness - do it at the right time
