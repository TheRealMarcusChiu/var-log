###### Small Form-factor Pluggable (SFP)

```excerpt
- introduced in 2001
- is a transceiver or cable with one or two lanes (channels) in each direction
- an SFP interface on networking hardware is a modular slot for a media-specific transceiver, such as a fiber-optic or copper cable
- the advantage of using SFPs compared to fixed interfaces is that individual ports can be equipped with different types of transceivers as required
- SFP transceivers exist supporting: [[802.8 - Fiber Optic|SONET]], [[802.3 - Ethernet|Ethernet]], [[Fibre Channel (FC)|Fibre Channel]], [[Passive Optical Network (PON)|PON]]
```

^excerpt
