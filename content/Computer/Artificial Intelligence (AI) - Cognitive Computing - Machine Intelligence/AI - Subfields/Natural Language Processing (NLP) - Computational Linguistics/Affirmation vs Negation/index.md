In [[Linguistics|linguistics]] and [[Grammar = Morphology + Part of Speech + Syntax|grammar]], <strong>affirmation</strong> and <strong>negation</strong> (<strong>aff</strong> and <strong>neg</strong>) are the ways that grammar encodes negative and positive polarity in verb phrases, clauses, or other utterances.

- <strong>affirmative</strong> (positive) form expresses the validity/truth of a basic assertion (e.g. "Jane is here")
- <strong>negative</strong> form expresses its falsity of a basic assertion (e.g. "Jane is not here")
