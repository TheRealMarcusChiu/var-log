The KDE community maintains multiple free-software projects. The project formerly referred to as <em>KDE</em> (or <em>[KDE SC (Software Compilation)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE_Software_Compilation)</em>) nowadays consists of three parts
- [[KDE Plasma - KDE Desktop (K Desktop Environment)|KDE Plasma]], a graphical desktop environment with customizable [layouts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Layout_(computing)) and panels, supporting [virtual desktops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_desktop) and widgets. Written with [Qt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qt_(software)) and [KDE Frameworks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE_Frameworks)
- [KDE Frameworks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE_Frameworks), a collection of [libraries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_(computing)) and [software frameworks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_framework) built on top of Qt (formerly known as 'kdelibs' or 'KDE Platform')
- [KDE Gear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE_Gear), utility applications (like [Kdenlive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kdenlive) or [Krita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krita)) mostly built on KDE Frameworks and which are often part of the official KDE Applications release
