# Types of Stories

1. <strong>Your Story</strong> – establishes your credibility and a bond with your audience before diving into your
2. <strong>Your Company’s Story</strong> – the story that tells how your company got started (Great for conferences)
3. <strong>Customer/Client Stories</strong> – highly effective method to not seem pushy about your products/services
4. <strong>Objection Destroyer Stories</strong> – useful when seeking buy-in from teams and customers
5. <strong>Study Story</strong> – makes scientific studies, data, and numbers more engaging and memorable
6. <strong>Teaching Stories</strong> – used to teach listeners lessons and leaves them with lasting points
7. <strong>Inspiring Stories</strong> – perfect for inspiring and motivating your audience to take action

# Quotes

- create an opponent... who is exceptionally good at attacking your hero's greatest weakness
- true character is revealed in choices a human being makes under pressure - the greater the pressure, the deeper the revelation, the truer the choice to the character's essential nature
- It is only by competing for the same goal that the hero and the opponent are forced to come into direct conflict and to do so again and again throughout the story
- don't always connect the dots. Give the audience a role in filling in what's happening. That is, give them all the dots they need but don't connect all the dots for them
