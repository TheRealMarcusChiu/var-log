<strong>Figures of Speech (FoS)</strong> are typically divided into 2 types:
- <strong>tropes</strong> - are FoS that deviates from the literal or ordinary meaning of words and phrases
- <strong>schemes</strong> - are rhetoric adjustments that make text read more smoothly

![[Figures of Speech (FoS)/Periodic-Table-Figures-Of-Speech.jpg]]
# <strong>Tropes</strong>
###### reference
- metaphor - reference of one thing to imply another
- simile - explicit comparison of 2 unlike things
- synecdoche - a part is used for a whole or a whole is used for a part
- metonymy - naming an object or concept to refer to another, related object or concept
- personification - referencing inanimate objects with human qualities or abilities
- anthropomorphism -

###### wordplay and puns
- antanaclasis - repetition of a word with each with different meanings
- paronomasia - use of words similar in sound but different in meaning (punning)
- syllepsis - use of the same word differently to modify two or more objects
- onomatopoeia - forming a word to imitate a sound

###### substitutions
- anthimeria - substitution of one part of speech for another
- periphrasis - circumlocution; use of a descriptive phrase or proper noun to stand for qualities of the phrase or noun

###### overstatement/understatement
- hyperbole - exaggeration for effect
- auxesis - use of a term to describe something disproportionately less significant than the term implies
- litotes - deliberate understatement
- melosis - use of a term to describe something disproportionately greater than the term implies

###### inversions
- rhetoric question - asking a question for a purpose other than to get an answer
- irony - use of term to convey a meaning opposite of the terms' literal meaning
- oxymoron - placing two opposing terms side-by-side
- paradox - contradictory phrase that contains some measure of truth

# <strong>Schemes</strong>
###### <strong>balance</strong>
- parallelism - similarity in structure between words and phrases
- antithesis - juxtaposing 2 contradictory ideas
- climax - ordering words and phrases in order of increasing importance

###### word order
- anastrophe - inversion of natural speaking word order
- parenthesis - insertion of terms or phrases that interrupt the natural syntactical flow
- apposition - addition of words to clarify or elaborate what came before

###### omission/inclusion
- ellipsis - omission of words implied by context
- asyndeton - omission of conjunctions between clauses
- brachylogia - omission of conjunctions between a series of words
- polysyndeton - an overabundance of conjunctions
