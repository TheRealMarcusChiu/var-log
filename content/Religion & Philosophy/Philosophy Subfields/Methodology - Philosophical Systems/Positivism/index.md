<strong>Positivism</strong> is a [philosophical theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophical_theory) stating that certain ("positive") knowledge is based on natural [[Phenomenon - Noumenon|phenomena]] and their properties and relations. Thus, information derived from sensory experience, interpreted through reason and logic, forms the exclusive source of all certain [[Knowledge|knowledge]]. Positivism holds that valid knowledge (certitude or truth) is found only in this [[A Priori Proposition - A Posteriori Proposition|a posteriori]] knowledge.

Verified data (positive facts) received from the senses are known as empirical evidence; thus positivism is based on [[Epistemology|empiricism]].

Positivism also holds that society, like the physical world, operates according to general laws. [Introspective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introspection) and [intuitive knowledge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intuitive_knowledge) is rejected, as are [metaphysics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphysics) and [theology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theology) because metaphysical and theological claims cannot be verified by sense experience.
