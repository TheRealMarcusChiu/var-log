```excerpt
- <strong>[[Metaphysics]]</strong> & <strong>[[Epistemology]]</strong>
	- <strong>Philosophy of Language</strong> ([[Philology - Historical-Comparative Linguistics|Philology]]) - explores the nature, the origins, and the use of language
	- <strong>Philosophy of Mind (Cognitive Science)</strong> - explores the nature of the mind and its relationship to the body. It is typified by disputes between [dualism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dualism_(philosophy_of_mind)) and [materialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materialism)
	- <strong>Philosophy of Human Nature</strong> - analyzes the unique characteristics of human beings, such as rationality, politics, and culture
		- <strong>[[Social Philosophy]]</strong> - examines society in terms of ethical values rather than empirical relations
	- <strong>Metaphilosophy</strong> - explores the aims of philosophy, its boundaries, and its methods
- ![[Axiology (Value Theory)#^excerpt]]
- <strong>[[Logic]] </strong>- the study of reasoning and argument
- <strong>[[Mathematics]]</strong>
- <strong><strong>[[Science]]</strong></strong>
- <strong>Philosophy of Science</strong> - explores the foundations, methods, history, implications, and purpose of science
- <strong>[[Philosophy of Religion]]</strong> - deals with questions that involve religion and religious ideas from a philosophically neutral perspective (as opposed to theology which begins from religious convictions)
- <strong>[[Phenomenology]] </strong>- the study of the structures of experience and consciousness
```
