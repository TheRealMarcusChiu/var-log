###### Ontology
- is the philosophical study of being
- a set of concepts and categories in a subject area or domain that shows their properties and the relations between them

Ontologists often try to determine what the categories or highest kinds are and how they form a system of categories that provides an encompassing classification of all entities. Commonly proposed categories include:
- substances
- properties
- relations
- states of affairs
- events

# Ontological Theories
- <strong>Mono-categorical Ontologies</strong> hold that there is only one basic category, which is rejected by <strong>Poly-categorical Ontologies</strong>.
- <strong>Hierarchical Ontologies</strong> assert that some entities exist on a more fundamental level and that other entities depend on them. <strong>Flat Ontologies</strong>, on the other hand, deny such a privileged status to any entity

# High Level Ontology of Sorts

<strong>Entity Types</strong>
- <strong>situation</strong> - anything that happens at a time and place
	- <strong>state </strong>- do not imply a change
	- <strong>event </strong>- imply a change in the status of other entities
- <strong>quantity </strong>- a few, two, 22
- <strong>object</strong>
	- <strong>concrete </strong>- occupy space, are touchable, tangible
		- <strong>animated </strong>- have life, vigor, spirit
		- <strong>in-animated </strong>- without life, dull
	- <strong>abstract</strong> - intangible, they are somehow product of human reasoning
		- <strong>temporal </strong> - last week, July
		- <strong>non-temporal </strong>- justice, pain, odor
	- <strong>composite object</strong> - an object composed of objects
- <strong>quality </strong>- heavy, difficult
- <strong>descriptor</strong> - complement entities by stating properties about their spatial or temporal context
	- <strong>temporal</strong> - by the end of the day, on July 15th
	- <strong>local </strong>- on the roof, near the stadium

### Categories
- categories are classes or sets of objects that have some common properties (e.g. basketballs, children, dogs)
- objects are members of category
- 2 ways of representing categories:
	- unary predicates (e.g. <code>basketball()</code> or <code>basketball(b)</code>)
	- reify the category as an object (e.g. basketballs or <code>member(b,Basketballs)</code>)
	- reification - the process of regarding something abstract as a material thing
- an object is a member of category
- a category can be a subclass of another category
- all members of a category have the same properties
- members of a category can be recognized by some properties
- a category as a whole has some properties
- inheritance is a property through which more specific concepts adopt the properties of more general concepts
	- <code><em>fuji apple <em>⊆ </em>apple ⊆ fruit ⊆ food</em></code>
