- [[Moral|morality]] means acting in conformity with accepted rules of right
- indignation is a feeling of righteous anger

### Moral Indignation

When we put the two words together, we see that moral or righteous indignation means acting angrily upon or through one's sense of right, or suited to act in such a manner; as a moral argument or moral consideration. Thus <strong>moral indignation</strong>, as someone has noted elsewhere, "would be anger felt about something (seen, done or said) that is morally wrong"
