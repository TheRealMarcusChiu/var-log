### <strong>Terms</strong>

- <strong>temple</strong> - a building devoted to the worship, or regarded as the dwelling place, of a god or gods or other objects of religious reverence
- <strong>tabernacle</strong>
  1. (in biblical use) a fixed or movable habitation, typically of light construction.
  2. a meeting place for worship used by some Protestants or Mormons
- <strong>church</strong> - a building used for public Christian worship
- <strong>cathedral</strong> - the principal church of a diocese, with which the bishop is officially associated
- <strong>mosque</strong> - a Muslim place of worship
- <strong>basilica</strong> - a large oblong hall or building with double colonnades and a semicircular apse, used in ancient Rome as a court of law or for public assemblies
  - a building similar to a Roman basilica, used as a Christian church
  - the name given to certain churches granted special privileges by the Pope
- <strong>pagoda</strong> - a Hindu or Buddhist temple or sacred building, typically a many-tiered tower, in India and East Asia
- <strong>monastery</strong> - a building or buildings occupied by a community of monks living under religious vows
- <strong>sepulcher</strong> - a small room or monument, cut in rock or built of stone, in which a dead person is laid or buried
- <strong>pyramid</strong> - a monumental structure with a square or triangular base and sloping sides that meet in a point at the top, especially one built of stone as a royal tomb in ancient Egypt
- <strong>mastaba</strong> - an ancient Egyptian tomb for bureaucracy officials. usually a forerunner of a pyramid
