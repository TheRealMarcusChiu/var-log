<strong>anthropic principle</strong> - the cosmological principle that theories of the universe are constrained by the necessity to allow human existence

The <strong>anthropic principle</strong> is a philosophical consideration that observations of the universe must be compatible with the [conscious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consciousness) and [sapient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sapience) life that observes it, and that there is hence a [survivorship bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Survivorship_bias). Proponents of the anthropic principle reason that it explains why this universe has the [age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_the_universe) and the [fundamental physical constants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimensionless_physical_constant) necessary to accommodate conscious life. As a result, they believe it is unremarkable that this universe has fundamental constants that happen to fall within the [narrow range thought to be compatible with life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fine-tuned_universe). The <strong>strong anthropic principle</strong> (<strong>SAP</strong>), as explained by [John D. Barrow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_D._Barrow) and [Frank Tipler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Tipler), states that this is all the case because the universe is in some sense compelled to eventually have conscious and sapient life [emerge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergence) within it. Some critics of the SAP argue in favor of a <strong>weak anthropic principle</strong> (<strong>WAP</strong>) similar to the one defined by [Brandon Carter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandon_Carter), which states that the universe's ostensible fine tuning is the result of [selection bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selection_bias) (specifically survivorship bias): i.e., only in a universe capable of eventually supporting life will there be living beings capable of observing and reflecting on the matter. Most often such arguments draw upon some notion of the [multiverse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiverse) for there to be a [statistical population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_population) of universes to select from and from which selection bias (our observance of <em>only</em> this universe, compatible with <em>our</em> life) could occur![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k5axKjJpT7Y)![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SAZAKPUQMw0)
