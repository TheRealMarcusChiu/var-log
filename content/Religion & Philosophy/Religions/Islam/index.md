### Jihad

The literal meaning of Jihad is struggle or effort, and it means much more than <strong>holy war</strong>.

Muslims use the word Jihad to describe three different kinds of struggle:

- a believer's internal struggle to live out the Muslim faith as well as possible
- the struggle to build a good Muslim society
- holy war: the struggle to defend Islam, with force if necessary

### Sunnis & Shiites

- <strong>sunnis</strong> - adheres to the Sunni branch of Islam
- <strong>shiites</strong> - adheres to the Shia branch of Islam

The group now known as <strong>Sunnis</strong> chose Abu Bakr, the prophet's adviser, to become the first successor, or caliph, to lead the Muslim state. <strong>Shiites</strong> favored Ali, Muhammad's cousin and son-in-law. Ali and his successors are called imams, who not only lead the <strong>Shiites</strong> but are considered to be descendants of Muhammad

A <strong>Shiite is</strong> a <strong>Muslim</strong> who follows specific religious traditions. <strong>Shiites are</strong> the second-largest branch of <strong>Islam</strong>, after Sunnis. A <strong>Shiite</strong> believes that Mohammed's son-in-law, Ali, was his legitimate successor as political and religious leader and prophet
