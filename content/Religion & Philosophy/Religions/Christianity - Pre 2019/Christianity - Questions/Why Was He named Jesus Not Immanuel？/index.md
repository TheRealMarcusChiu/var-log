### Matthew 1:21-23

"Mary will give birth to a son, and you are to give him the name Jesus, because he will save his people from their sins (the name Jesus is the Greek form of Joshua, which means 'the Lord saves'). All this took place to fulfill what the Lord had said through the prophet (Isaiah): 'The Virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and they will call him Immanuel' (which means 'God with us')"

### Why was Jesus called Jesus instead of Immanuel?

3 Reasons:

- That was what the angel-of-the-Lord commanded
- Immanuel means "God with us". The prediction of Isaiah was in reference to who the Messiah would be. John 1:14 - "The Word (who is God - John 1:1) became flesh and dwelt among us" Jesus, who is the Word that became flesh. The Word that came from the mouth of God. The very same mouth Who spoke and the heavens and earth appeared. The Word who was with God in the beginning who became flesh (took human form) and lived among the people, just as the title Immanuel indicates
- Jesus had many names, such as Messiah (Hebrew) and Christ (Greek) which both mean "Anointed One" Isaiah 9:6 - "For unto us a child is born, unto us a son was given; and the government shall be upon his shoulders, and His Name shall be called, Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace"

As we now know, Jesus has several names. But He shall be called Jesus. Jesus meaning "the Lord saves" as Matthew 1:21 says, "His name shall be Jesus, because he will save his people from their sins". As you continue to read Matthew, Jesus laid down His life to die for our sins. As it was His Father's will: to bring the world back to Himself through Christ. that God be with us - Immanuel

His name is very special. Jesus Himself says we should pray like this, "Our Father in heaven, hallowed be thy name..." We should be filled with wonder, awestruck wonder, at the mention of His Name. Paul explains this very well in Philippians 2:9-11 - "Therefore God exalted him to the highest place and gave him the name that is above every name, that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth, and every tongue acknowledge that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father". Reading this now, has taken me by surprised where it says, "every knee should bow, in HEAVEN and on earth and UNDER the earth" think about that for a minute. And that every tongue acknowledge that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father. This includes the angels in heaven, "Day and night they never stop saying: “Holy, Holy, Holy, is the Lord God Almighty, who was and is and is to come!" ~ Revelation 4:8
