<strong>Matthew 11:25</strong>

At that time Jesus said, “I praise you, Father, Lord of heaven and earth, because you have hidden these things from the wise and learned, and revealed them to little children. 26 Yes, Father, for this is what you were pleased to do.

<strong>Matthew 13:10-17</strong>

The disciples of Jesus asked him, why do you speak to the crowds in parables? He answered to you it has been given to you to know the secrets of the kingdom of heaven and to them it is not given. To he who has more will be given, and he will have an abundance. To he who has not, even what he has will be taken. Jesus spoke to them in parables because: seeing they do not see, hearing they do not hear, they do not understand.

Then this was to fulfill what was prophesied in Isaiah: (note how in the end it says healing is not done until you see, hear, understand and turn) what I found profound is the word "turn".

A couple of versus come into mind:
- "Deny yourself pick of the cross and follow Him"
- James 2:19 that says, "even the devils believe that God is one, and tremble/shudder"

Repent is a word that also comes up. Maybe it is the action of your turning.

"Seeing but do not see, hearing but do not hear" reminds me of Jesus doing miracles. He healed the deaf and blind. I don't know if there is an actual correlation but by doing this on the outside earthly blind and deaf healing he could also heal the spiritually blind and deaf. With this in mind, Matthew 13:58, "And he did not do many miracles there because of their lack of faith"

Now that begs the question, what does Jesus mean when he says, hearing and seeing. My first guess would be faith. But in Matthew 13:17 it says many prophets and righteous people longed to see what you see and did not hear, longed to hear what you hear and did not hear. Maybe it is seeing Jesus in person and hearing Him. But the crowds Jesus was speaking to see Him in person and hear Him in person. Maybe the crowds do not perceive he is the prophesied Messiah even when He is in person.
