# Wisdom

There is a mine for silver and a place where gold is refined... 
<strong>But where can wisdom be found?</strong> Where does understanding dwell? ...
The fear of the Lord - that is wisdom, and to shun evil is understanding.
\~ Job 28

<strong>[Does not](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/3808.htm)[wisdom](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/2451.htm)</strong>[<strong>call out</strong>,](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/7121.htm)[and understanding](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/8394.htm)[raise](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/5414.htm)[her voice?](https://biblehub.com/hebrew/6963.htm)
\~ Proverbs 8:1

Choose my instruction instead of silver, knowledge rather than choice gold, for wisdom is more precious than rubies, and <strong>NOTHING you desire can compare with her</strong>.
\~ Proverbs 8:9-10

Proverbs 2:1-5 teaches that if you listen to and treasure wisdom, you will understand the fear of the Lord and find the knowledge of God.

1 My son, if you accept my words
    and store up my commands within you,
2 <strong>turning your ear to wisdom</strong>
    and applying your heart to understanding—
3 indeed, if you call out for insight
    and cry aloud for understanding,
4 and if you look for it as for silver
    and search for it as for hidden treasure,
5 <strong>then you will understand the fear of the Lord</strong>
    and find the knowledge of God.
6 For the Lord gives wisdom;
    from his mouth come knowledge and understanding.

# Fear - Knowledge/Wisdom

The <strong>fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge</strong>, but fools despise wisdom and instruction
\~ Proverbs 1:7

The <strong>fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom</strong>, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding. For through wisdom your days will be many, and years will be added to your life.
\~ Proverbs 9:10-11
# Fear - Evil

Do not be wise in your own eyes; <strong>fear the Lord and shun evil</strong>. This will bring health to your body and nourishment to your bones.
\~ Proverbs 3:7

<strong>To fear the Lord is to hate evil</strong>; I hate pride and arrogance, evil behavior and perverse speech.
\~ Proverbs 8:13

By loving devotion and faithfulness iniquity is atoned for, and <strong>by the fear of the LORD one turns aside from evil</strong>.
\~ Proverbs 16:6

Moses said to the people, “Do not be afraid. God has come to test you, so that <strong>the fear of God will be with you to keep you from sinning</strong>.”
\~ Exodus 20:20

Surely his <strong>salvation is near those who fear him</strong>, that his glory may dwell in our land.
\~ Psalms 85:9

If you, O Lord, kept track of iniquities, then who, O Lord, could stand? <strong>But with You there is forgiveness, so that You may be feared</strong>.
\~ Psalm 130:3-4
Lord, if you kept a record of our sins, who, O Lord, could ever survive? <strong>But you offer forgiveness, that we might learn to fear you.</strong>
\~ Psalm 130:3-4

Therefore, my beloved, just as you have always obeyed, not only in my presence, but now even more in my absence, continue to <strong>work out your salvation with fear and trembling. Fo it is God who works in you</strong> to will and to act on behalf of His good purpose.
\~ Philippians 2:12-13

For I will forgive their iniquities and will remember their sins no more
\~ Hebrews 8:12

The <strong>gospel is the difference between being afraid of God and fearing God</strong>, as it transforms a servile, cowering fear into a filial, reverential, and humble fear rooted in love and adoration.
