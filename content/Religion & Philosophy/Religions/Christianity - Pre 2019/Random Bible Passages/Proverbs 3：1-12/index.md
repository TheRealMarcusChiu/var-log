VERSE 1-2

- how does keeping His commandments prolong our life for many years?
- well let's look at the first commandment of the Bible: it is "Do not eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, or you will surely die"
- clearly there is some correlation there
- but, then you could be the person who asks, "why would we surely die if we eat from the tree?"
- the answer is I don't really know, I'll ask you this, why did God tell them to march around the walls of Jericho? It doesn't logically make any sense marching around and sounding our trumpets. But they did it and in the end God's way always works. We just need to have FAITH that His Word never fails.
- have faith and LOVE in keeping His COMMANDMENTS reminds me of John 14:15 and 21

VERSE 3-4

- Let love and faithfulness never leave you; bind them around your neck and write them on the tablet of your heart.
- everywhere you go, every step you take, you walk by faith and with love
- then you will win favor and a good name in the sight of God and man
- with faith and love bounded around your neck, you will become like a cistern overflowing for the people around you: reminds me of John 15, if you abide in Him you will bear fruits
- write them on the tablet of your heart, reminds me of Jeremiah 31:33 - This is the covenant I will make with the people of Israel after that time," declares the Lord. "I will put my law in their minds and write it on their hearts. I will be their God, and they will be my people.

VERSE 5-8

- The highlight of Proverbs 3
- Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make you paths straight
- do not be wise in your own eyes; fear the Lord and shun evil. This will bring health to your body and nourishment to your bones

VERSE 9-10

- "the FIRSTFRUITS of all your crops" reminds me of Abel's offering - Genesis 4:4
- "your BARNS will be filled to overflowing" reminds me of the Parable of the Rich Fool - Luke 12:13-21

VERSE 11-12

- reminds me John 15 (the 2 things that stuck out to me):
  - He prunes those who bear fruit so they bear even more fruit
  - abide in Him and you will bear fruit
- pruning rose bushes: every year a rose bush is pruned:
  - it helps prevent disease
  - it limits the number of buds so not all of them would empty out the nutrients and thus all die
    - have you ever felt like there's too much you want to accomplish, yet there's not enough time?
- also remind me of 2 Timothy 3:16-17
  - All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, 17 so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work
- we should mimic that as well, rebuke those who we love, prune their "disease" which ultimately builds them up
- Jeremiah 1:9-10 - Behold, I have put my words in your mouth. 10 See, I have set you this day over nations and over kingdoms, to pluck up and to break down, to destroy and to overthrow, to build and to plant
- Ephesians 4:29 - Do not let any unwholesome talk come out of your mouths, but only what is helpful for building others up according to their needs, that it may benefit those who listen.
