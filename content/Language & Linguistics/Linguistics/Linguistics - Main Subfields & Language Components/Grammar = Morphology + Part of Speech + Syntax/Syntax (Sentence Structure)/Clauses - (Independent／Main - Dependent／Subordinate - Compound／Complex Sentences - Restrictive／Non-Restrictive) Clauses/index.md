      "- are not used as modifiers; they perform the functions that a noun performs\n- <strong>subject noun clause</strong> - functions as the entire [[Subject - Predicate (Verb - Object)|subject]] of the sentence, for example:\n\t- <code>\\[What the chairman proposed\\] was not practical</code>\n\t- <code>\\[That their house is for sale\\] is a well-known fact</code>\n- <strong>object noun clause</strong> - functions as the object\n\t- <code>I hope \\[that you will be promoted\\]</code>\n\t- <code>Tell the manager \\[why you are leaving\\]</code>\n- <strong>predicate noun clause</strong> - functions as the entire [[Subject - Predicate (Verb - Object)|predicate]]\n\t- <code>The rumor was \\[that he had left the city\\]</code>\n\t- <code>This is \\[that we agreed to do\\]</code>\n- <strong>object-predicate noun clause</strong> - functions as objects of a [[Adpositions (Prepositions - Postpositions - Circumposition)|preposition]]\n\t- <code>Give the message to \\[whoever is in the office\\]</code>\n\t- <code>We did not agree about \\[what the doctor ordered\\]</code>\n- <strong>appositives noun clause</strong> - functions as appositives (appositives are words placed near other words to explain them)\n\t- <code>Mike, \\[our janitor\\], is very accommodating</code>\n\t- <code>We called Dr. Hughes, \\[a famous scientist\\]</code>\n\t- <code>\\[My brother\\] Andrew is in London</code>\n\t- <code>The rumor \\[that John would be elected\\] spread rapidly</code>\n\t- <code>The fact \\[that the contract was signed\\] was important</code>"
