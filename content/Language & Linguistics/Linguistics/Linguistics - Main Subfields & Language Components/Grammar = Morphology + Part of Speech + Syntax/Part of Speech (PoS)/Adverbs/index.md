###### Adverbs
- modifies or qualifies an adjective, or other adverb or word group verbs
- for example: <em><code>Unfortunately</code>, John walked home <code>extremely slowly</code> yesterday</em>
	- <code>extremely</code> - modifies adverb slowly
	- <code>slowly</code> - modifies verb walked
	- <code>Unfortunately</code> - modifies the whole sentence

### Adverb - Position

one of the hallmarks of adverbs is their ability to move around in a sentence
- <code>Solemnly</code> the minister addressed the gathering
- The minister addressed the gathering <code>solemnly</code>
- The minister <code>solemnly</code> addressed the gathering

### Adverb - Types
- <strong>directional adverbs</strong> or <strong>locative adverbs</strong> - <em>here, home, downhill</em>
- <strong>degree adverbs</strong> - <em>extremely, very, somewhat, enough, nearly</em>
- <strong>manner adverbs</strong> - <em>slowly, slinkily, delicately</em>
- <strong>temporal adverbs</strong> - <em>yesterday, Monday</em>
- <strong>frequency adverbs</strong> - <em>always, usually, never, hardly, sometimes</em>
- <strong>opinion adverbs</strong> - <em>actually, certainly apparently, clearly, obviously</em>
