<strong>adpositions</strong> (or broadly, in English, simply <strong>prepositions</strong>), are a [[Morphology (Word Forms／Inflexions)|class of words]] used to express spatial or temporal relations (<em>in</em>, <em>under</em>, <em>towards</em>, <em>before</em>) or mark various semantic roles (<em>of</em>, <em>for</em>)
### Types of Adpositions
A adposition typically combines with a [[Nouns|noun]] or [[Pronouns|pronoun]], or more generally a [noun phrase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noun_phrase), this being called its [complement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complement_(grammar)), or sometimes [object](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Object_(grammar)). 
- a <strong>preposition</strong> comes before its complement
- a <strong>postposition</strong> comes after its complement
- a <strong>circumposition</strong> consists of two parts that appear on each side of the complement
- a <strong>ambiposition</strong>
- a <strong>inposition</strong>
- a <strong>interposition</strong>
