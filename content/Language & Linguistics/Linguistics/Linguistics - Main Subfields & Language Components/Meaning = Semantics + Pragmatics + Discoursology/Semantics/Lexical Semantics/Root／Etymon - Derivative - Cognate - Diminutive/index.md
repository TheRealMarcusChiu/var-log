- <strong>root</strong>
  - ([[Morphology (Word Forms／Inflexions)|linguistic morphology]]) the primary lexical unit of a word, which carries the most significant aspects of semantic content and cannot be reduced into smaller constituents. Inflectional stems often derive from roots.
  - ([[Philology - Historical-Comparative Linguistics|philology]]) a word from which another word or words are derived
- <strong>etymon</strong> - a word or morpheme from which a later word is derived
- <strong>derivative</strong> - a word derived from a root
- <strong>cognate</strong> - words derived from the same root
- <strong>diminutive</strong> - a shortened form of a name, typically used informally
