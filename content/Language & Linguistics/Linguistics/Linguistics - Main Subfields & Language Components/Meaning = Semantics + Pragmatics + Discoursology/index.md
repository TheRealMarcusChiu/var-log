- <strong>pragmatics</strong>
	- is the study of sentence meaning with environmental context (goals and intentions of the speaker) includes: prosody, body language, etc
	- is an even broader field that studies how the environmental context of a sentence contributes to meaning—for example, someone shouting “Fire!” has a very different meaning if they are in charge of a seven-gun salute than it does if they are sitting in a crowded movie theater.
- <strong>perspectives</strong>
	- a basic problem is that if John says <code><font style="color: rgb(128,128,128);">This is fun</font></code> and Mary says <code><font style="color: rgb(128,128,128);">This is not fun</font></code>, it's possible for both sentences simultaneously to be true (relative to their respective speakers), but we also want to claim that John and Mary are overtly contradicting or disagreeing with each other
- <strong>[[Hermeneutics - Exegesis|hermeneutics]]</strong> - specifically deals with the interpretation of literary texts
