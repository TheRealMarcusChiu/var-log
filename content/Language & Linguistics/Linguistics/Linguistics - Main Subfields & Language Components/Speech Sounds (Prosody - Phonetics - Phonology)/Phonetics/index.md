###### Phonetics
- is a branch of [[Linguistics|linguistics]] that studies how humans make and perceive sounds, or in the case of sign languages, the equivalent aspects of sign

### Notes - TODO
- Most human speech takes place on expirations alone, interspersed with rapid inspirations
- when primates produce a long sequence of sounds, it is based on a series of both expirations and inspirations. This is in contrast to human speech in which multiple units of sounds are produced during an extended expiration. In nonhuman primates, breathing rate imposes a limit on call duration and calling rate. There may, however, be another way around this limitation: air sacs
- in stark contrast to humans, the frontal cortex of nonhuman primates has little or no role in the production of vocalizations and, by extension, little voluntary control of their communicative behavior. Unfortunately, this topic has not been pursued in earnest for decades now, but there is a strong sense among many investigators that it needs to be revisited
