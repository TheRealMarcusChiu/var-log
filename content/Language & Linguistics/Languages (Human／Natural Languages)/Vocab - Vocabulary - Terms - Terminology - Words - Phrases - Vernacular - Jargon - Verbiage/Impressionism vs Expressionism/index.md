- <strong>impressionism</strong> - captures the essence of a scene through careful use of light (impressionists paint the scene as if they had only glanced at it for a moment)
- <strong> expressionism</strong> - uses vivid colors to convey the artist's subjective emotional response to that object (e.g. using disproportionate sizes, odd angles, and painted in vivid and intense colors)
