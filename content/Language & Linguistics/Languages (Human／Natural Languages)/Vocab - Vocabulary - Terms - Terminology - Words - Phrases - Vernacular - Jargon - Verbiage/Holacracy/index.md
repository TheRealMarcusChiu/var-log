<strong>Holacracy</strong> is a method of decentralized management and organizational [governance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporate_governance), in which authority and decision-making are distributed throughout a [holarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holarchy) of self-organizing teams rather than being vested in a management hierarchy.

founder Brian Robertson distilled the company's best practices into an organizational system that became known as Holacracy in 2007. Robertson later developed the "[Holacracy Constitution](http://holacracy.org/constitution)", which lays out the core principles and practices of the system. In June 2015, he released the book <em>Holacracy: The New Management System for a Rapidly Changing World,</em> that details and explains his practices. The term holacracy is derived from the term [holarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holarchy), coined by [Arthur Koestler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arthur_Koestler) in his 1967 book <em>[The Ghost in the Machine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Ghost_in_the_Machine)</em>. A holarchy is composed of [holons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holon_(philosophy)) (Greek: ὅλον, holon neuter form of ὅλος, holos "whole") or units that are autonomous and self-reliant, but also dependent on the greater whole of which they are part. Thus a holarchy is a hierarchy of self-regulating holons that function both as autonomous wholes and as dependent parts.
