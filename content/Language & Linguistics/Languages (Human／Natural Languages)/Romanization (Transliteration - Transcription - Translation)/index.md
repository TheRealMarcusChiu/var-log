<strong>Romanization</strong>

- romanization is the conversion of a writing system to the [[Latin Alphabet (Roman Alphabet)|Roman/Latin Script]]
- romanization is not the same as translation, as translation is the communication of the <strong>meaning</strong> of a <em>source-language</em> text by means of an equivalent<em>target-language</em> text

### Methods of Romanization

- <strong>transliteration</strong>
  - literal one-to-one mapping of characters in the source language to target language
  - less emphasis on how the result sounds when pronounced according to the reader's language
- <strong>transcription</strong> - for representing the spoken word
  - <strong>phonemic transcription</strong>
    - mapping of phonemes (units speech sounds attached to language) from source language to target language
    - resulting in greater pronunciation integrity than transliteration
  - <strong>phonetic transcription (see: [[International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)]])</strong>
    - mapping of phones (units of speech sounds detached from language) from source language to target language
    - resulting in greater pronunciation integrity than phonemic transcription
- <strong>hybrid</strong> - combinations of both: transliteration and transcription

### Translation (Not the Same as Transliteration nor Transcription)

- <strong>transliteration </strong>is the communication of <strong>syntax</strong> at the level of individual characters (not words)
- <strong>transcription</strong> is the communication of <strong>speech sounds</strong>
- <strong>translation</strong> is the communication of the <strong>[[Meaning = Semantics + Pragmatics + Discoursology|meaning/semantics]]</strong> of a <em>source-language</em> text by means of an equivalent<em>target-language</em> text
