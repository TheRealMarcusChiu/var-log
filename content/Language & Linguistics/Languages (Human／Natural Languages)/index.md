###### Language
````excerpt
- is the method of human communication, either spoken or written, consisting of the use of words in a structured and conventional way
- is a system that consists of the development, acquisition, maintenance and use of complex systems of communication, particularly the human ability to do so; and a language is any specific example of such a system. The scientific study of language is called linguistics
````
^excerpt

# Language - Classifications
- <strong>living language</strong> - alanguage which is still spoken in the contemporary period
- <strong>dead language</strong> - a language which is still in use but is no longer the native language of any community
- <strong>extinct language</strong> - a language that no longer has any speakers, especially if the language has no living descendants
- <strong>endangered language</strong> or <strong>moribund language </strong>- a living language that is at risk of becoming a dead or even extinct language

- <strong>attested languages</strong> - are languages (living or dead) that have been documented and for which the evidence (attestation) has survived to the present day. Evidence may be recordings, transcriptions, literature or inscriptions
- <strong>unattested languages</strong> - purported languages with no direct evidence

- <strong>proto-language</strong> - can be thought of as a mother language being the root which all languages in the family stem from. see [[Philology - Historical-Comparative Linguistics|philology]]
