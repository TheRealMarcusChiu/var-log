The <em><strong>dakuten</strong></em> ( ﾞ)(Japanese: 濁点, Japanese pronunciation: [\[dakɯ̥teɴ\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Japanese), lit. "voicing mark"), colloquially <em>ten-ten</em> (点々, "dots"), is a [[diacritical marks - diacritic|diacritic sign]] most often used in the Japanese kana syllabaries to indicate that the consonant of a syllable should be pronounced voiced, for instance, on sounds that have undergone rendaku (sequential voicing).

The <em><strong>handakuten ( ﾟ)</strong></em> (半濁点, Japanese pronunciation: [\[handakɯ̥teɴ\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Japanese), lit. "half voicing mark"), colloquially <em>maru</em> (丸, "circle"), is a diacritic used with the kana for syllables starting with <em>h</em> to indicate that they should instead be pronounced with \[p\].
