###### M0 M1 M2 M3 Money Supply
````excerpt
- M0 refers to the most liquid form of money: cash. That includes central bank notes and coins
- M1 includes all cash, and demand deposits (e.g. checking accounts)
- M2 includes all cash, demand deposits, and other types of deposits that are readily convertible to cash (e.g. savings account, certificate of deposit CDs)
- M3 includes M2 money as well as large time deposits, institutional money market funds, short-term repurchase agreements, and larger liquid funds
````
^excerpt

- MB = cash and coins in circulation + bank reserves
- M1 = cash and coins in circulation + checkable bank deposits + travelers checks
- M2 = M1 + savings accounts + small CDs + money market mutual funds
