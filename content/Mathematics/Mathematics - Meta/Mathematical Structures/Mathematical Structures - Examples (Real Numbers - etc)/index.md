# Mathematical Structures - Example \#1 On Real Numbers

The set of [[Numbers Classes／Classification of Numbers|real numbers]] has several standard [[Mathematical Structures|mathematical structures]]:
- <strong>an order</strong>: each number is either less or more than any other number.
- <strong>[[Algebraic Structures|algebraic structure]]</strong>: there are operations of multiplication and addition that make it into a [[Algebraic Structures|field]].
- <strong>a measure</strong>: intervals along the real line have a specific length, which can be extended to the [Lebesgue measure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebesgue_measure) on many of its subsets.
- <strong>a metric</strong>: there is a notion of distance between points.
- <strong>a geometry</strong>: it is equipped with a metric and is flat.
- <strong>a topology</strong>: there is a notion of open sets.

There are interfaces among these:
- Its order and, independently, its metric structure induces its topology
- Its order and [[Algebraic Structures|algebraic structure]] make it an [ordered field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordered_field)
- Its algebraic structure and topology make it into a [Lie group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_group), a type of [topological group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topological_group)
