###### number e (Euler's number)

- is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 2.71828...
- can be characterized in many ways:
  - it is the base of the natural logarithm
  - it is the summation of infinite series:
    - 𝑒 = 𝛴<sub>0≤𝑥≤∞</sub>\[1/𝑥!]
  - it is the limit of (1 + 1/𝑥)<sup>𝑥</sup> as 𝑥 approaches infinity (an expression that arises in the study of [compound interest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compound_interest))
    - 𝑒 = 𝑙𝑖𝑚<sub>𝑥→∞</sub>(1 + 1/𝑥)<sup>𝑥</sup>
    - 𝑒<sup>𝑎</sup> = 𝑙𝑖𝑚<sub>𝑥→∞</sub>(1 + 𝑎/𝑥)<sup>𝑥</sup>
    - 𝑒<sup>-𝑎</sup> = 𝑙𝑖𝑚<sub>𝑥→∞</sub>(1 - 𝑎/𝑥)<sup>𝑥</sup>
  - it is the limit of (1 + 𝑥)<sup>1/𝑥</sup> as 𝑥 approaches 0
    - 𝑒 = 𝑙𝑖𝑚<sub>𝑥→0</sub>(1 + 𝑥)<sup>1/𝑥</sup>
  - ## it is also the unique positive number 𝑎 such that the [[Power／Exponential Function - Logistic Function|exponential function]] 𝑓(𝑥) = 𝑎<sup>𝑥</sup> has a slope of 1 at 𝑥 = 0
