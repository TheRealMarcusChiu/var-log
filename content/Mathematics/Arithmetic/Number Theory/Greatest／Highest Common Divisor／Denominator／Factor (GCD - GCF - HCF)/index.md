In [[Mathematics|mathematics]], the <strong>greatest common divisor</strong> (<strong>gcd</strong>), <strong>greatest common denominator</strong>, <strong>greatest common factor</strong> (<strong>gcf</strong>), or <strong>highest</strong> <strong>common factor (hcf)</strong>, of two or more non-zero integers, is the largest positive integer that divides the numbers without a remainder.

### For Example

- <strong>GIVEN:</strong> 8 & 12
- <strong>GCD:</strong> 4
