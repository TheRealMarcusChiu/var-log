In [[Number Theory|number theory]], two integers<em>a</em> and <em>b</em> are said to be <strong>relatively prime</strong>, <strong>mutually prime</strong>, or <strong>coprime</strong> (also written <strong>co-prime</strong>) if the only positive [[Integer Factorization - Prime Factorization|integer factor]] that divides both of them is 1.
