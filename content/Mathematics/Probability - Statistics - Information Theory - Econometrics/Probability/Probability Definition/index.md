###### 4 Views on Probabilities Complement Each Other

If you know that a physical process produces certain outcomes with certain [[Probability as Long-Term Frequency|long-term frequencies]], it’s natural to think that the process has the [[Probability as Physical Propensity／Tendency|physical propensity]] to produce these outcomes with that frequency. Then it’s also natural to adjust your [[Probability as Degrees of Belief|degrees of belief]] (expectations) for each outcome, so they match that propensity. Or you can go the other way around and form your expectations for the frequency of occurrence of an outcome (by appealing to [[The Law of Large Numbers|the law of large numbers]]) after a [[Probability as Degrees of Logical Support|logical analysis]] of the sample space.
