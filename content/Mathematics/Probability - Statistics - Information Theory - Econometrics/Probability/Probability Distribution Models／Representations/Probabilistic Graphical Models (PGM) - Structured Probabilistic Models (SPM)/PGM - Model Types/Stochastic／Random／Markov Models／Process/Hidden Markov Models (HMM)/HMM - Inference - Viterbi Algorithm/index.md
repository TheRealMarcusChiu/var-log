### Problem Definition

given:
- a trained [[Hidden Markov Models (HMM)|Hidden Markov Model (HMM)]]
- an observation sequence {𝑥<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑥<sup>(𝑇)</sup>}

determine the most likely hidden-state sequence {𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>}

- {𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>} = 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>∊𝑌</sub> ... 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>∊𝑌</sub> <font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">𝐏(𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>,<font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">𝑥</font><sup>(1)</sup><font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">, ..., 𝑥</font><sup>(𝑇)</sup>) / <font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">𝐏(</font><font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">𝑥</font><sup>(1)</sup><font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">, ..., 𝑥</font><sup>(𝑇)</sup><font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">)</font></font>
- <font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);"><font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);"><font style="color: rgb(34,34,34);">{𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>} = 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>∊𝑌</sub> ... </font><font style="color: rgb(34,34,34);">𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>∊𝑌</sub> <font style="color: rgb(128,0,0);">𝐏(𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>,𝑥<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑥<sup>(𝑇)</sup>)</font></font></font></font>

### Viterbi Algorithm Solution

Viterbi Algorithm computes a trellis using dynamic programming

observation is processed from left to right filling out a trellis of states

<font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">𝑣(𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)</font><sup>(𝑡)</sup> is the probability that HMM is in state 𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub> after seeing the first 𝑡 observations:
- <font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">𝑣(𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)</font><sup>(𝑡)</sup>= 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>∊𝑌</sub> ... 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(𝑡-1)</sup>∊𝑌</sub> 𝐏(𝑥<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑥<sup>(𝑡)</sup>,𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑡-1)</sup>,𝑠<sup>(𝑡)</sup>=𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)
- <font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">𝑣(𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)</font><sup>(𝑡)</sup>= 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sub>𝑖</sub></sub><sub>∊𝑌</sub> \[<font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">𝑣(𝑠<sub>𝑖</sub>)</font><sup>(𝑡-1)</sup>·<font style="color: rgb(204,153,255);">𝐏(𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>|𝑠<sub>𝑖</sub>)</font>·<font style="color: rgb(153,204,255);">𝐏(𝑥<sup>(𝑡)</sup>|𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)</font>\] <font style="color: rgb(128,128,128);">\# for 𝑡 \> 1</font>
- <font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">𝑣(𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)</font><sup>(1)</sup>= <font style="color: rgb(255,153,0);">𝐏(𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>)</font>·<font style="color: rgb(153,204,255);">𝐏(𝑥<sup>(1)</sup>|𝑠<sub>𝑗</sub>) <font style="color: rgb(128,128,128);">\# for 𝑡 = 1</font></font>

{𝑠<sup>(1)</sup>, ..., 𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup>} = 𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑚𝑎𝑥<sub>𝑠<sup>(𝑇)</sup></sub><sub>∊𝑌</sub>\[<font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">𝑣(𝑠</font><font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);"><sup>(𝑇)</sup></font><font style="color: rgb(128,0,128);">)</font>\]
