###### Lattice Random Walk
- is a type of [[Random Walk]] on a regular lattice, where at each step the location jumps to another site according to some [[Probability Distributions|probability distribution]]

# Lattice Random Walk - Variants
- <strong>simple random walk</strong> - the location can only jump to neighboring sites of the lattice, forming a lattice path
- <strong>simple symmetric random walk</strong> - the probabilities of the location jumping to each one of its immediate neighbors are the same. The best-studied example is of random walk on the 𝑑-dimensional integer lattice (sometimes called the hypercubic lattice) ℤ<sup>𝑑</sup>
- <strong>simple bordered symmetric random walk</strong> - the state space is limited to finite dimensions, and the transition probabilities depend on the location of the state because on margin and corner states the movement is limited
