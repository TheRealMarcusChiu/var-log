# Finite Exchangeability
- a finite [[Set／Sets|set]] {𝑦<sub>1</sub>, 𝑦<sub>2</sub>, ..., 𝑦<sub>𝑛</sub>} is <strong>exchangeable</strong> if the [[Joint Probability Distribution (Compound - Bivariate - Multivariate - Full)|joint probability]] 𝐏(𝑦<sub>1</sub>, 𝑦<sub>2</sub>, ..., 𝑦<sub>𝑛</sub>) is invariant under [[Permutations - Combinations - Total Combination|permutations]] of the indices
- that is, for every permutation (𝜔<sub>1</sub>, 𝜔<sub>2</sub>, ..., 𝜔<sub>𝑛</sub>) of {𝑦<sub>1</sub>, 𝑦<sub>2</sub>, ..., 𝑦<sub>𝑛</sub>}; 𝐏(𝑦<sub>1</sub>, 𝑦<sub>2</sub>, ..., 𝑦<sub>𝑛</sub>) = 𝐏(𝜔<sub>1</sub>, 𝜔<sub>2</sub>, ..., 𝜔<sub>𝑛</sub>)

# Infinite Exchangeability
- is the property that every finite subset of an infinite sequence {𝑦<sub>1</sub>, 𝑦<sub>2</sub>, ...} is exchangeable
