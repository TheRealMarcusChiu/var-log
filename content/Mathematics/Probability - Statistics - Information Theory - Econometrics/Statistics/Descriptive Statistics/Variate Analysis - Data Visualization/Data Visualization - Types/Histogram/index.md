a <strong>Histogram</strong> is an accurate representation of the [[Probability Distributions|distribution]] of numerical data (continuous or discrete). Its shape is similar to the population [[Probability Distribution Function Variants - Probability／Mass／Density／Cumulative／Survivor／Hazard／Cumulative-Hazard／Inverse／Moment-Generating Distribution Functions|probability density function or probability mass function]], especially if the sample size is large.
# Histogram - Types
- <strong>Frequency Histogram</strong> - consists of bars, one over each bin, whose heights represent the number of observations in the bins
- <strong>Relative Frequency Histogram</strong> - consists of bars, one over each bin, whose heights represent the proportion of observations in the bins
- <strong>Probability (Density) Histogram</strong> - area of a bar equals the probability of observations in that bin
