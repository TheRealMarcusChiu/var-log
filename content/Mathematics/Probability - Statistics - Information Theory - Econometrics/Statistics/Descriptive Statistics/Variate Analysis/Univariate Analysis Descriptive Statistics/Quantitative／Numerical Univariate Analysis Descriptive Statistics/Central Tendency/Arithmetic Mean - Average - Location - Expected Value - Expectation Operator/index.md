- <strong>Mean ([[Raw Moments - Moments About the Origin／Zero - Central／Mean Moments - Moments About The Mean - (Zeroth／First／Second／Third／Fourth／kth／nth) -  of a Probability Density Function|First Raw Moment]]) / Average (𝜇 or 𝑋̅)</strong>
	- is a type of [[Central Tendency]]
	- is the sum of all values divided by the total number of values
	- is the balance point of a distribution
- <strong>Expected Value / Expectation (𝐄</strong>\[\]<strong>)</strong>
	- a predicted value of a variable, calculated as the sum of all possible values each multiplied by the probability of its occurrence
	- is a property of a variable (either random or not random)
	- calculating expectation of [[Continuous Distribution - Calculating (Expected Value - Expectation - Mean - Average)|continuous distribution]] & [[Discrete Probability Distribution - Calculating Statistics|discrete distribution]]

# Syntax

When "E" is used to denote "expected value", authors use a variety of stylizations:
- the expectation operator can be stylized as <strong>𝐄</strong> (bold upright), 𝐸 (italic), or 𝔼 (blackboard bold)
- the expectation of 𝑋 can be stylized as 𝐸(𝑋), 𝐸\[𝑋\], 𝐸𝑋, 𝜇<sub><em>𝑋</em></sub>, whereas ⟨𝑋⟩, ⟨𝑋⟩<sub>𝑎𝑣</sub>, and 𝑋̅ are commonly used in physics, and 𝑀(𝑋) in Russian-language literature.
