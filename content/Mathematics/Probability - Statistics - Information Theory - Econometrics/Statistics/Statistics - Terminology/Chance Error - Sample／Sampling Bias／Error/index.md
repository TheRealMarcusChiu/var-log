- <strong>chance error</strong>
- <strong>sampling bias</strong> is a [bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bias_(statistics)) in which a sample is collected in such a way that some members of the intended population have a lower or higher [sampling probability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sampling_probability) than others
- <strong>sampling errors </strong>are caused by the mere fact that only a sample, a portion of a population, is observed. For most of reasonable statistical procedures, sampling errors decrease (and converge to zero) as the sample size increases
- <strong>non-sampling errors </strong>are caused by inappropriate sampling schemes or wrong statistical techniques. Often no wise statistical techniques can rescue a poorly collected sample of data
- <strong>bias</strong> or <strong>systematic error</strong> -

individual measurement = exact value + bias + chance error:
- exact value - constant
- bias - constant
- chance error - random variable
