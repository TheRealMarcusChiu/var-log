# Statement

Every 𝑛×𝑛 matrix has exactly 𝑛 [[Complex Eigenvalues - Complex Eigenvectors|complex eigenvalues]], counted with multiplicity
# Proof

To compute the complex eigenvalues of a matrix we solve the [[Characteristic Polynomial|characteristic polynomial]] of that matrix.

The [[Fundamental Theorem of Algebra]] states that every polynomial equation of degree 𝑛 with [[Complex Numbers System|complex number]] coefficients has 𝑛 roots, or solutions, in the complex numbers. The roots can have a multiplicity greater than zero
