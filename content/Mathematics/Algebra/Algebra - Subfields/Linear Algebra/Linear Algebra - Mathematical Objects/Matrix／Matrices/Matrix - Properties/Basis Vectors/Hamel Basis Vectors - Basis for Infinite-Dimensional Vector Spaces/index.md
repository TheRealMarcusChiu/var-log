# Basis for Infinite-Dimensional Vector Spaces
###### ARTICLE
[[infinite-dimensional-basis-vectors.pdf]]
###### SUMMARY

Every vector space has a basis, that is, a maximal linearly independent subset. Every vector in a vector space can be written in a unique way as a finite linear combination of the elements in this basis. A basis for an infinite dimensional vector space is also called a Hamel basis. The proof that every vector space has a basis uses the axiom of choice. There is no practical way to find a Hamel basis in general, which means we have little use for the concept of a basis for a general infinite (especially uncountable) dimensional vector space.
