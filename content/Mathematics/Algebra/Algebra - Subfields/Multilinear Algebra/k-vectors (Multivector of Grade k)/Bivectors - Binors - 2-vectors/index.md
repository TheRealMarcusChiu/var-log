###### Bivectors - Binors - 2-vectors
````excerpt
- is a quantity in [[Exterior Algebra - Grassmann Algebra|exterior algebra]] or [[Geometric Algebra (GA) - Clifford Algebra|geometric algebra]] that extends the idea of [[Scalars|scalars]] and [[Vectors|vectors]]
- is a type of [[k-vectors (Multivector of Grade k)|k-vector]] where k=2
- Today the <em>bivector</em> is largely studied as a topic in [[Geometric Algebra (GA) - Clifford Algebra|geometric algebra, a Clifford algebra]] over [[Real Vector Spaces|real]] or [[Complex Vector Spaces|complex vector spaces]] with a [[Quadratic Forms|quadratic form]]
- just as the [[Standard Basis Vectors|standard basis vectors]] are formed by unit vectors, so is the standard basis bivectors are formed by unit planes
- just as 1-vectors are projections of a position in space onto the basis vectors, so are 2-vectors projections of an area in space onto basis bivectors
- does NOT encode position in the associated vector space
- When we take the wedge product of 2 vectors result is, 𝐮∧𝐯 = (𝑢<sub>1</sub>𝑣<sub>1</sub><sub></sub>+ 𝑢<sub>2</sub>𝑣<sub>2</sub>)(𝐞<sub>1</sub>∧𝐞<sub>2</sub>)
	- scalar part is the area of the parallelepiped (i.e. 𝑢<sub>1</sub>𝑣<sub>1</sub><sub></sub>+ 𝑢<sub>2</sub>𝑣<sub>2</sub>)
	- bivector part is the "direction" of that area (i.e. 𝐞<sub>1</sub>∧𝐞<sub>2</sub>)
````
^excerpt
