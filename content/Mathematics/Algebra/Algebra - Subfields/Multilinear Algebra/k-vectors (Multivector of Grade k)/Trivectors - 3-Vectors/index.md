###### Trivectors - 3-Vectors

```excerpt
- is a [[k-vectors (Multivector of Grade k)|k-vector]] where k=3
- When we take the wedge product of 3 vectors result is, 𝐮∧𝐯∧𝐰 = (𝑢<sub>1</sub>𝑣<sub>2</sub>𝑤<sub>3</sub>+ 𝑢<sub>2</sub>𝑣<sub>3</sub>𝑤<sub>1</sub> + 𝑢<sub>3</sub>𝑣<sub>1</sub>𝑤<sub>2</sub>− 𝑢<sub>1</sub>𝑣<sub>3</sub>𝑤<sub>2</sub> − 𝑢<sub>2</sub>𝑣<sub>1</sub>𝑤<sub>3</sub> − 𝑢<sub>3</sub>𝑣<sub>2</sub>𝑤<sub>1</sub>)(𝐞<sub>1</sub>∧𝐞<sub>2</sub>∧𝐞<sub>3</sub>)
	- scalar part is the volume of the parallelepiped (i.e. 𝑢<sub>1</sub>𝑣<sub>2</sub>𝑤<sub>3</sub>+ 𝑢<sub>2</sub>𝑣<sub>3</sub>𝑤<sub>1</sub> + 𝑢<sub>3</sub>𝑣<sub>1</sub>𝑤<sub>2</sub>− 𝑢<sub>1</sub>𝑣<sub>3</sub>𝑤<sub>2</sub> − 𝑢<sub>2</sub>𝑣<sub>1</sub>𝑤<sub>3</sub> − 𝑢<sub>3</sub>𝑣<sub>2</sub>𝑤)
	- trivector part is the "direction" of that volume (i.e. 𝐞<sub>1</sub>∧𝐞<sub>2</sub>∧𝐞<sub>3</sub>)
```
