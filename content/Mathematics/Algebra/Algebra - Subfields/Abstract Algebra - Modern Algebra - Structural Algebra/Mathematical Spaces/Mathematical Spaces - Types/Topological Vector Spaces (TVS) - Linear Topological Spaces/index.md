###### Topological Vector Spaces (TVS) - Linear Topological Spaces (𝑉,𝐹,𝜏)
````excerpt
- is a type of [[Mathematical Spaces|mathematical space]]
- is both a [[Vector Spaces - Linear Spaces|vector space (𝑉,𝐹)]] and [[Topological Spaces|topological space (𝑋,𝜏)]] where 𝑉=𝑋 and with the property that the vector space operations (i.e. vector addition and scalar multiplication) are also [[Continuous／Continuity [at a point - everywhere] (Transformations／Operations／Operations／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms)|continuous functions]]
- the [[Topology|topology (𝜏)]] is called a [[Vector Topology|vector topology (𝜏𝑉)]]
- every TVS has a [uniform topological structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uniform_space), allowing a notion of [uniform convergence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uniform_convergence) and [completeness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complete_topological_vector_space)
- some authors also require that the space is a [Hausdorff space](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hausdorff_space)
````
^excerpt
