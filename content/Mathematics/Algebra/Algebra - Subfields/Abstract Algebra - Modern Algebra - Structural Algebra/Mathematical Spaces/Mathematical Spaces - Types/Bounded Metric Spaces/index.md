###### Bounded Metric Spaces (𝑋,𝑑)

```excerpt
- is a type of [[Mathematical Spaces|mathematical space]]
- is a type of [[Metric Spaces|metric space]] that is [[Metric Spaces (Epsilon Balls - Open Sets - Boundary Points／Sets - Closed Sets - Closures - Bounded Sets ／ Boundedness - Unbounded Sets ／ Unboundedness - Totally Bounded Sets ／ Totally Boundedness)|bounded]]

	- thus any subset of 𝑋 is a [[Metric Spaces (Epsilon Balls - Open Sets - Boundary Points／Sets - Closed Sets - Closures - Bounded Sets ／ Boundedness - Unbounded Sets ／ Unboundedness - Totally Bounded Sets ／ Totally Boundedness)|bounded set]]
```

# Bounded Metric Spaces - Examples

- \[0,1]<sup>3</sup> "unit cube" is a bounded metric space
- \[0,1]<sup>𝑛</sup> of any finite dimension is a bounded metric space
- \[0,1]<sup>∞</sup> of infinite dimension is a bounded metric space (but not [[Metric Spaces (Epsilon Balls - Open Sets - Boundary Points／Sets - Closed Sets - Closures - Bounded Sets ／ Boundedness - Unbounded Sets ／ Unboundedness - Totally Bounded Sets ／ Totally Boundedness)|totally bounded]])

# Bounded Metric Spaces - Non-Examples

- ℝ<sup>3</sup> is NOT a bounded metric space
- ℝ<sup>𝑛</sup> is NOT a bounded metric space
- ℝ<sup>∞</sup> is NOT a bounded metric space
