# Commutativity/Commutative - Definition

An [[Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms|operation ∗]] on a [[Set Theory|set 𝑆]] is called <em>commutative</em> if:

- 𝑥 [[Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms|∗]] 𝑦 = 𝑦 [[Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms|∗]] 𝑥       for all 𝑥,𝑦 ∊ 𝑆

An operation that does not satisfy the above property is called <em>non-commutative</em>.
