###### Diffeomorphic/Diffeomorphisms

```excerpt
- <em>diffeomorphism</em> is an [[Function - Injective／Injection／One-to-one - Surjective／Surjection／Unto／Onto - Bijective／Bijection／One-to-one Correspondence|invertible/bijective function]] that maps one [[Differentiable／Differential Manifold|differentiable manifold]] to another. Both the [[Functions (Domain - Codomain - Preimage - Image - Range)|function]] and its [[Inverse Function - Anti-Function|inverse function]] are differentiable
- <em>diffeomorphisms</em> are [[Morphisms - Homomorphic／Homomorphisms (Isomorphic／Isomorphisms - Monomorphic／Monomorphisms／Injective - Epimorphic／Epimorphisms／Surjective - Endomorphic／Endomorphisms - Automorphic／Automorphisms)|isomorphisms]] of smooth manifolds, they are not just continuous but also preserve the differential structure
- two manifolds are said to be <em>diffeomorphic</em> if there exists a <em>diffeomorphism</em> between them
```
