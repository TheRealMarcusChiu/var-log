# Continuous/Continuity - Definitions
- [Continuity (mathematics)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuity_(mathematics)) - is the opposing concept to discreteness; common examples include
	- [[Probability Distribution - Continuous Functions／Models (Probability Density Functions)|Continuous Probability Distribution]] - or random variable in probability and statistics
	- [Continuous Game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuous_game) - a generalization of games used in game theory
	- [Law of Continuity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_continuity) - a heuristic principle of Gottfried Leibniz
- [[Continuous／Continuity [at a point - everywhere] (Transformations／Operations／Operations／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms)|Continuous Functions]] - in particular:
	- [[Continuous Functions／Transformations Between Topological Spaces (Topological Continuity - Topologically Continuous)|Topological Continuity]] - for functions between [[Topological Spaces|topological spaces]]
	- [[Transformations Between Metric Spaces (Ordinary Continuity ／ Continuous [at a point - everywhere] - Uniform／Uniformly／Sequential／Sequentially／Hölder／Lipschitz Continuous／Continuity)|Sequential Continuity]] - for functions between [[Metric Spaces|metric spaces]]
	- [Scott Continuity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scott_continuity) - for functions between posets
	- [Continuity (Set Theory)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuity_(set_theory)) - for functions between ordinals
	- [Continuity (Category Theory)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuity_(category_theory)) - for functors
	- [Graph Continuity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graph_continuity) - for payoff functions in [[Game Theory|game theory]]
- Continuity Theorem - may refer to one of two results:
	- [Lévy's continuity theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9vy%27s_continuity_theorem), on random variables
	- [Kolmogorov continuity theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolmogorov_continuity_theorem), on [[Stochastic／Random／Markov Models／Process|stochastic processes]]
- In [[Geometry]]:
	- [Parametric Continuity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parametric_continuity), for parametrized curves
	- [Geometric Continuity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometric_continuity), a concept primarily applied to the conic sections and related shapes
- In [[Probability Theory]]:
	- [[Continuous-Valued Continuous-Time Stochastic Process|Continuous Stochastic Process]]
