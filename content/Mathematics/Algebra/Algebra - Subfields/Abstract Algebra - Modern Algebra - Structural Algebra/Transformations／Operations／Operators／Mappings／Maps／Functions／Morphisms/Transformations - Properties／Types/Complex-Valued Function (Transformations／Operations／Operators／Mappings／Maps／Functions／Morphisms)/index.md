# Complex-Valued Function - Definition

For any complex function, the values 𝑧 from the domain and their images 𝑓(𝑥) in the range may be separated into [real](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_number) and [imaginary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imaginary_number) parts:

- 𝑧 = 𝑥 + 𝑖𝑦
- 𝑓(𝑧) = 𝑓(𝑥 + 𝑖𝑦) = 𝑢(𝑥,𝑦) + 𝑖𝑣(𝑥,𝑦)

where 𝑥, 𝑦, 𝑢(𝑥,𝑦), 𝑣(𝑥,𝑦) are all real-valued

In other words, a complex function 𝑓:ℂ→ℂ may be decomposed into:

- 𝑢:ℝ<sup>2</sup>→ℝ
- 𝑣:ℝ<sup>2</sup>→ℝ

i.e., into two real-valued functions (𝑢, 𝑣) of two real variables (𝑥, 𝑦)
