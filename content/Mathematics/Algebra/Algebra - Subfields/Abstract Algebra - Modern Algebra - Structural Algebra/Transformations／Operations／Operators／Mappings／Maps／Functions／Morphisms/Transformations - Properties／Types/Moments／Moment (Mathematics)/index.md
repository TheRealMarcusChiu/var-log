###### Moments/Moment
````excerpt
- The <strong>moments</strong> of a [[Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms|function]] are certain quantitative measures related to the shape of the function's graph
- If the function represents mass density, then:
	- the zeroth moment is the total mass, the first moment (normalized by total mass) is the [center of mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Center_of_mass)
	- the second moment is the [moment of inertia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moment_of_inertia)
- The moments of a [[Probability Distributions|probability distribution]] are called [[Raw Moments - Moments About the Origin／Zero - Central／Mean Moments - Moments About The Mean - (Zeroth／First／Second／Third／Fourth／kth／nth) -  of a Probability Density Function|raw moments and central moments]]
- The mathematical concept is closely related to the concept of [moment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moment_(physics)) in physics.
````
^excerpt
