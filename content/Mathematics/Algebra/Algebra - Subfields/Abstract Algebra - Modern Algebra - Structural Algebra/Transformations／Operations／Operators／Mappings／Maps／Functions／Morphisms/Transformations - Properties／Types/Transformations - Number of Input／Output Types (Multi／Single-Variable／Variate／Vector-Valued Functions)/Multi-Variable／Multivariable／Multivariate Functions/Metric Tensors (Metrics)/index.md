###### Metric Tensor (Metric) - 𝑔(·,·)

```excerpt
- a <em>metric tensor</em> is an additional structure on a [[Manifolds (n-Dimensional Manifold (n-Manifold)|manifold]] 𝑀 that allows defining distances and angles, just as the [[Dot Product - Scalar Product - Canonical／Euclidean／Standard Inner Product|standard inner product]] on a [[Euclidean Vector Spaces - Euclidean Spaces|Euclidean space]] allows defining distance and angles there)
- a <em>metric tensor</em> at a point 𝑝 of 𝑀 is a [[Bilinear Functionals - Bilinear Forms - 2／Two Forms|bilinear form]] defined on the [[Tangent Spaces - Tangent Vectors|tangent space]] at 𝑝 (that is, a [[Bilinear (Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms)|bilinear function]] that maps pairs of [[Tangent Spaces - Tangent Vectors|tangent vectors]] to [[Real Numbers System|real numbers]])
- a <em>metric tensor</em> on 𝑀 consists of a <em>metric tensor</em> at each point 𝑝 of 𝑀 that varies smoothly with 𝑝
- is a (0,2)-[[Tensors|tensor]]
- is an example of a [[Tensor-Valued Functions - Tensor Fields|tensor field]]
- the components of a <em>metric tensor</em> in a [coordinate basis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coordinate_basis) take on the form of a [[Symmetric Matrix|symmetric matrix]] whose entries transform [covariantly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Covariance_and_contravariance_of_vectors) under changes to the coordinate system. Thus a metric tensor is a covariant [symmetric tensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetric_tensor)
- a manifold equipped with a [[Positive／Negative Semi Definite／Definiteness Indefinite Functions|positive-definite]] <em>metric tensor</em> is known as a [[Riemannian Manifolds - Riemannian Spaces|Riemannian manifold]]
```

^excerpt
