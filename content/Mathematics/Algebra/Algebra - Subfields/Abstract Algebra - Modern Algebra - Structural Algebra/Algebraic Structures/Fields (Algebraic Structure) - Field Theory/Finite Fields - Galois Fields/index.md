# Finite Fields - Properties

Every field 𝐹 has 𝑞=𝑝<sup>𝑛</sup> elements, where 𝑝 is prime and 𝑛≥1. This statement holds since 𝐹 may be viewed as a vector space over its prime field. The dimension of this vector space is necessarily finite, say 𝑛, which implies the asserted statement.

A field with 𝑞=𝑝<sup>𝑛</sup> elements can be constructed as the splitting field of the polynomial:
- 𝑓(𝑥) = 𝑥<sup>𝑞</sup> - 𝑥
