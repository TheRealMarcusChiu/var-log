###### Field of Sets
````excerpt
- is a mathematical structure consisting of a pair (𝑋,𝐹) where:
	- 𝑋 is a set
	- 𝐹 is a family of subsets of 𝑋 called an algebra over 𝑋 following the conditions:
		- 𝐹 contains the empty set as an element; ∅∊𝐹
		- 𝐹 is closed under the complement of 𝑋; if {𝑥}∊𝐹, then 𝑋\\{𝑥}∊𝐹
		- 𝐹 is closed under finite unions; if {𝑥}∊𝐹 and {𝑦}∊𝐹, then {𝑥}⋃{𝑦}∊𝐹
		- 𝐹 is closed under finite intersections; if {𝑥}∊𝐹 and {𝑦}∊𝐹, then {𝑥}⋂{𝑦}∊𝐹
- field of sets play an essential role in the representation theory of [[Boolean Algebra|Boolean algebra]]. Every Boolean algebra can be represented as a field of sets
- if 𝐹 is also closed under countable unions and/or intersections, then 𝐹 is also a [[σ-Algebra - σ-Field of Sets - σ-field|σ-algebra]]
````
