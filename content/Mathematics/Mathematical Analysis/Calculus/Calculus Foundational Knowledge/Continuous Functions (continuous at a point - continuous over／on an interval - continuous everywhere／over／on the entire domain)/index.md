###### Continuous Functions

```excerpt
- a [[Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms|function]] is <strong>continuous at point</strong> 𝑝 if its [[Limits - Limit of a Function|limit]] to 𝑝 equals its [[Calculus Foundational Knowledge|evaluation]] at 𝑝, i.e.:
	- $\lim_{x \to p}f(x) = f(p)$

- a function is <strong>continuous (everywhere or over the entire domain)</strong> if it is continuous at every point
	- $\lim_{x \to p}f(x) = f(p) \;\;\;\;\;\; \text{for all } p \in \text{domain}$
- functions that are not continuous are [[Discontinuous Functions (Point／Removable - Jump - Asymptotic Discontinuities／Discontinuity)|discontinuous functions]]
```
