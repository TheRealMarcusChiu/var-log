# Surface Integrals of Scalar Fields

Given a [[Scalar-Valued Functions - Scalar Fields|scalar-valued function]] 𝑓 and surface 𝑆. The <em>surface integral</em> of 𝑓 over 𝑆 is defined as:
- $∬_S f \; dS = ∬_T f(𝐫(s,t)) \; \| \frac{∂𝐫}{∂s} ⨯ \frac{∂𝐫}{∂t} \| \; ds \; dt$

where:
- 𝐫(𝑠,𝑡) is the parametrization of 𝑆
- (𝑠,𝑡) varies in some region 𝑇 in the plane
- ||·|| is the magnitude of the [[Cross Product - Vector Product - Directed Area Product|cross-product]] of the [[Derivative of Scalar-Valued Function (Partial Derivative - Total Derivative - Gradient - Directional Directive - Second Order Partial Derivative)|partial derivatives]] of 𝐫(𝑠,𝑡)
