###### Guass's Divergence Theorem

```excerpt
- The divergence theorem states that the [[Surface Integral|surface integral]] of a [[Vector-Valued Functions - Vector Fields|vector-valued function]] over a closed surface, which is called the "flux" through the surface, is equal to the [[Volume Integral|volume integral]] of the [[Divergence (𝛻· ｜ 𝛻·𝑓)|divergence]] over the region inside the surface
- Intuitively, it states that "the sum of all sources of the field in a region (with sinks regarded as negative sources) gives the net flux out of the region"
```

^excerpt
