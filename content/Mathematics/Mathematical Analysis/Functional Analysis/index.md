###### Functional Analysis

```excerpt
- is a branch of [[Mathematical Analysis|mathematical analysis]], the core of which is formed by the study of [[Vector Spaces - Linear Spaces|vector spaces]] endowed with some kind of limit-related structure (e.g. [[Inner Products|inner product]], [[Norms - Semi-Norms|norm]], [[Topology|topology]], etc.) and the [[Linear Algebra - Operations／Operators|linear operators]] acting upon these spaces and respecting these structures in a suitable sense
- the historical roots of <em>functional analysis</em> lie in the study of spaces of functions and the formulation of properties of transformations of functions such as the [[Fourier Transform - Inverse Fourier Transform|Fourier transform]] as transformations defining continuous, unitary, etc. operators between [[Function Spaces|function spaces]]. This point of view turned out to be particularly useful for the study of differential and integral equations
```

^excerpt
