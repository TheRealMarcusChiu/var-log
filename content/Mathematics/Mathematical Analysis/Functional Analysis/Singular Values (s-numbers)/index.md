###### Singular Values (s-numbers)
````excerpt
- the <em>singular values</em>, or <em>s-numbers</em> of a [[Compact Operator (Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions)|compact operator (𝑇 : 𝑋 → 𝑌)]]acting between [[Hilbert Spaces|Hilbert spaces]] 𝑋 and 𝑌, are the square roots of the (necessarily non-negative) [[Eigenvectors (Characteristic Vectors) - Eigenvalues (Spectrum)|eigenvalues]] of the [[Self-Adjoint Operator (Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions)|self-adjoint operator (𝑇†𝑇)]] (where [[Self-Adjoint Operator (Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions)|𝑇†]] denotes the [[Hermitian Adjoint／Conjugate (Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions)|hermitian adjoint]] of 𝑇)
- the <em>singular values</em> of 𝑇 are non-negative real numbers, usually listed in decreasing order (𝜎<sub>1</sub>(𝑇), 𝜎<sub>2</sub>(𝑇), …)
- the largest <em>singular value</em> of 𝑇 is equal to the [[Operator Norm|operator norm]] of 𝑇
````
