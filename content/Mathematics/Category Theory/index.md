###### Category Theory
````excerpt
- <strong>category theory</strong> formalizes [[Mathematical Structures|mathematical structures]] (e.g. [[Algebraic Structures|algebraic structures]], topological structures, differential structures, etc) and their concepts in terms of a labeleddirected graph (called a <em>category) containing:</em>
	- a set of nodes ([[Mathematical Object|mathematical objects]]<em>)</em>
	- a set of labeled directed edges (<em>arrows</em> or [[Morphisms - Homomorphic／Homomorphisms (Isomorphic／Isomorphisms - Monomorphic／Monomorphisms／Injective - Epimorphic／Epimorphisms／Surjective - Endomorphic／Endomorphisms - Automorphic／Automorphisms)|morphisms]])
- <strong>category theory</strong> seeks to generalize all of mathematics in terms of <strong>categories</strong>, independent of what their objects and arrows represent
- informally, <strong>category theory</strong> is a general theory of [[Transformations／Operations／Operators／Mappings／Maps／Functions／Morphisms|functions]]
- a <strong>category</strong> has two basic properties:
	- the ability to compose the arrows associatively
	- the existence of an identity arrow for each object
````
