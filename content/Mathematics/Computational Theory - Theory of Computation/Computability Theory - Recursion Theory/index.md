###### Computability Theory
- is also known as <strong>recursion theory</strong>
- is a branch of mathematical logic and the [[Computational Theory - Theory of Computation|theory of computation]] that originated in the 1930s with the study of [computable functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computable_function) and [Turing degrees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_degree). The field has since expanded to include the study of generalized [computability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computability) and [definability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Definable_set). In these areas, computability theory overlaps with [proof theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proof_theory) and [effective descriptive set theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effective_descriptive_set_theory)

Basic questions addressed by computability theory include:
- What does it mean for a [[Functions (Domain - Codomain - Preimage - Image - Range)|function]] on [[Numbers Classes／Classification of Numbers|natural numbers]] to be computable?
- How can non-computable functions be classified into a hierarchy based on their level of non-computability?
