###### Asymptotic Geometry

```excerpt
- is the local theory of [[Banach Spaces|Banach spaces]]
- is a field of mathematics that investigates the geometric properties of finite-dimensional objects, such as [convex bodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_body) and [[Normed Vector Spaces - Normed Spaces|normed spaces]], as the dimension tends to infinity
- it is at the intersection of [[Convex Geometry|convex geometry]] and [[Functional Analysis|functional analysis]]
- the primary objects of study are typically [[Finite-Dimensional Normed Vector Spaces|finite-dimensional normed spaces]], which can be represented as ℝ<sup>𝑛</sup> equipped with a norm ‖⋅‖, or equivalently, a unit ball K<sub>X</sub>= { x ∈ ℝ<sup>𝑛</sup>: ‖x‖ ≤ 1 }, which is a [centrally symmetric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point_reflection), [compact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compact_set), [convex set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_set) with a non-empty [interior](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interior_(topology))
```

^excerpt
