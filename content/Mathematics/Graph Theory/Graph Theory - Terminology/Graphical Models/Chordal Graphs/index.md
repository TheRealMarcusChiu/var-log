A <strong>chordal graph</strong> is a [simple graph](http://mathworld.wolfram.com/SimpleGraph.html) in which every [graph cycle](http://mathworld.wolfram.com/GraphCycle.html) of length four and greater has a [cycle chord](http://mathworld.wolfram.com/CycleChord.html). In other words, a chordal graph is a graph possessing no [chordless cycles](http://mathworld.wolfram.com/ChordlessCycle.html).

The numbers of simple chordal graphs on n = 1, 2, 3, 4, ... nodes are 1, 2, 4, 10, 27, 94, 393, ...
