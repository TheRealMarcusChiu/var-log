###### Lie Theory
````excerpt
- the foundation of <strong>Lie theory</strong> is the [exponential map](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exponential_map_(Lie_theory)) relating [Lie algebras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_algebra) to [Lie groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_group) which is called the [Lie group–Lie algebra correspondence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_group%E2%80%93Lie_algebra_correspondence). The subject is part of [differential geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_geometry) since Lie groups are [[Differentiable／Differential Manifold|differentiable manifolds]]. Lie groups evolve out of the identity (1) and the [tangent vectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangent_vector) to [one-parameter subgroups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-parameter_group) generate the Lie algebra. The structure of a Lie group is implicit in its algebra, and the structure of the Lie algebra is expressed by [root systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Root_system) and [root data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Root_datum).
````
