###### Cryogenic Electron Microscopy (Cryo-EM)
- is a [cryomicroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryomicroscopy) technique applied on samples cooled to cryogenic temperatures and embedded in an environment of [vitreous water / amorphous ice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitreous_water)
- has attracted wide attention to the approach as an alternative to [[X-ray Crystallography]] or [NMR Spectroscopy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NMR_spectroscopy) for macromolecular structure determination without the need for crystallization

# Cryo-EM - Limitations
- Below 1 Å ([[Metric System - International System of Units (SI Units)|angstrom]]) is almost impossible to reach for cryo-EM
- Sub-1.5-Å, or even sub-2-Å, resolution performance will remain accessible for some time to <strong>ONLY</strong> well-behaved samples
