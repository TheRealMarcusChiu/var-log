###### Carbohydrates

- carbon (<em>carbo</em>-) + water (-<em>hydrate</em>)
- are roughly a ratio of 1 carbon atom (C) to 1 water molecule (H<sub>2</sub>O)
- classes of carbohydrates:
  - [[Monosaccharides|monosaccharides]]
  - [[Monosaccharides|disaccharides]] - 2 linked monosaccharides
  - [[Monosaccharides|oligosaccharides]] - 3 to 10 linked monosaccharides
  - [[Monosaccharides|polysaccharides]] - 10+ linked monosaccharides

# <strong>Disaccharides</strong>

<strong>disaccharides</strong> form when two monosaccharides join together via a [[Biological Molecules & Compounds|dehydration reaction]] forming a covalent bond known as a <strong>glycosidic linkage</strong>

### Types of Disaccharides

- <strong>lactose</strong> - glucose + galactose
- <strong>sucrose</strong> - glucose + fructose
- <strong>maltose</strong> - glucose + glucose
- <strong>trehalose</strong> - glucose + glucose

### Importance of Linkage

- <strong>lactose </strong>- 1-4 glycosidic linkage, the 1 carbon of galactose is connected to the 4 carbon of glucose
- <strong>sucrose</strong> - 1<font style="color: inherit;">-</font>2 glycosidic linkage, the 1 carbon of glucose is connected to the 2 carbon of fructose
- <strong>maltose</strong> - 1-4 glycosidic linkage, the 1 carbon of glucose is connected to the 4 carbon of glucose
- <strong>trehalose</strong> - 1-1 glycosidic linkage, the 1 carbon of glucose is connected to the 1 carbon of glucose

<strong>oligosaccharides</strong> - a chain of 3 to 10 monosaccharides

# <strong>Polysaccharides</strong>

<strong>polysaccharide </strong>- a long chain of 10+ monosaccharides linked by <strong>glycosidic bonds</strong>

### Types of Polysaccharides

- <strong>storage polysaccharides:</strong>
  - <strong>starch </strong>- storage form of glucose in plants
    - <strong>amylose </strong>- consists entirely of unbranched chains of glucose monomers connected by 1<font style="color: inherit;">-</font>4 linkages
    - <strong>amylopectin</strong> - is a branched polysaccharide. monomers are connected by mostly 1<font style="color: inherit;">-</font>4 linkages, with some 1<font style="color: inherit;">-</font>6 linkages that results in branched points
    - <strong>modified starches</strong> -
  - <strong>glycogen </strong>- storage form of glucose in humans and other vertebrates
- <strong>structural polysaccharides:</strong>
  - <strong>chitin</strong> - a major component of exoskeletons of arthropods (e.g. insects and crustaceans)
  - <strong>cellulose</strong> - a major component of plant cell walls
