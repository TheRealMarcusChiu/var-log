# <strong>Monoglyceride</strong>

- also: acylglycerol or monoacylglycerol
- monoglycerides are formed with 1 [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|glycerol]] and 1 [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|fatty acid]] by a process called dehydration synthesis (1 glycerol + 1 fatty acid ↔ 1 monoglyceride + H<sub>2</sub>O)
- 2 types of monoglycerides:
  - 1-monoacylglycerols where the fatty acid is attached to a primary alcohol
  - 2-monoacylglycerols where the fatty acid is attached to the secondary alcohol

# <strong>Diglyceride</strong>

- also:diacylglycerol (DAG)
- diglycerides are formed with 1 [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|glycerol]] and 2 [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|fatty acids]]  by a process called dehydration synthesis (1 glycerol + 2 fatty acids ↔ 1 diglyceride + 2H<sub>2</sub>O)
- 2 types of diglycerides:
  - 1,2-diacylglycerols
  - 1,3-diacylglycerols

# <strong>Triglyceride</strong>

- also: triacylglycerol
- triglycerides are considered fats
- triglycerides are formed with 1 [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|glycerol]] and 3 [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|fatty acids]] by a process called dehydration synthesis (1 glycerol + 3 fatty acids ↔ 1 triglycerides + 3H<sub>2</sub>O)
- triglycerides are stored in fat cells

# <strong>Types of Glycerides</strong>

- <strong>saturated fat</strong> - saturated with the most amount of Hydrogen atoms allowed
- <strong>unsaturated fat</strong> - not saturated
  - <strong>mono-unsaturated fat </strong>- just one double bond in a fatty acid
  - <strong>poly-unsaturated fat </strong>- multiple double bonds in a fatty acid
- see [[Functional Groups|Alkanes vs Alkenes]] for structure difference between saturated and unsaturated
- <strong>trans fat </strong>- contains [[Isomers|trans configuration]] instead of [[Isomers|cis configuration]]
