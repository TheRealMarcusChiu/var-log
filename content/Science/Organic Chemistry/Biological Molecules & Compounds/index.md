<strong>biological molecules and compounds</strong> are naturally occurring [[Molecules - Compounds|molecules and compounds]]

<strong>macromolecules</strong>, big (<em>macro-</em>) molecules made through the joining of smaller subunits

<strong>polymers </strong>- are long chains made up of repeating molecular subunits or monomers
<strong>monomers </strong>- are building blocks of polymers
### 4 Main Types of Biological Monomers
- <strong>[[Monosaccharides|monosaccharides]]</strong> - forms larger molecules called <strong>carbohydrates</strong>
- <strong>[[Nucleotides|nucleotides]]</strong> - forms larger molecules called <strong>nucleic acids</strong>
- <strong>[[Amino Acids|amino acids]]</strong> - forms larger molecules called <strong>proteins</strong> or <strong>peptides</strong>
- [[Glycerol & Fatty Acids|glycerol and fatty acids]] - forms larger molecules called <strong>lipids</strong>

# <strong>How Polymers are Built From Monomers - Dehydration Synthesis</strong>

often via <strong>dehydration synthesis</strong> reactions - which one monomer forms a covalent bond to another monomer, releasing a water molecule in the process

<strong>general form:</strong> monomer + monomer → polymer + H<sub>2</sub>O
<strong>monosaccharide form:</strong> monosaccharide + monosaccharides → disaccharide + H<sub>2</sub>O

# <strong>How Polymers are Broken Down to Monomers - Hydrolysis</strong>

often via <strong>hydrolysis</strong> reactions - which a bond is broken, or lysed, by addition of a water molecule

<strong>general form:</strong> polymer + H20 → monomer + monomer
<strong>monosaccharide form:</strong> disaccharide + H<sub>2</sub>O → monosaccharide + monosaccharides

# <strong>How to Speed Up Reactions - Enzymes</strong>
enzymes catalyze, or speed up, both the dehydration synthesis and hydrolysis reactions

enzymes involved in breaking bonds are often given names that end with -<em>ase</em>

example enzymes:
- maltase enzyme breaks down maltose
- lipases break down lipids
- peptidases break down proteins
