- <strong>Visual Encoding </strong>similar to structural encoding
- <strong>Structural Encoding</strong> focuses on what words look like. For instance, one might note whether words are long or short, in uppercase or lowercase, or handwritten or typed.
- <strong>Phonemic Encoding</strong> focuses on how words sound.
- <strong>Semantic Encoding</strong> focuses on the meaning of words. Semantic encoding requires a deeper level of processing than structural or phonemic encoding and usually results in better memory
