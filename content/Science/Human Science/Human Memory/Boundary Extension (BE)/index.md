<strong>Boundary Extension (BE)</strong> is a cognitive psychology phenomenon and an error of commission in which people remember more of a scene or boundary than was originally present in the original picture

our prior knowledge/beliefs/experiences dictates our extrapolation of a scene

below we are given 2 images. A subject was given an image to look at for a couple seconds then was told to redraw by memory
