###### Intelligence
- is general cognitive problem-solving skills
- is what you do when you don't know what to do
- is a hypothetical idea that we have defined as being reflected by certain types of behavior
- involves the ability to adapt to one’s environment and the capacity to learn from experience

# Theories of Intelligence
### Howard Gardner's Specific Intelligence 1983
- 8 specific Intelligence
	- <strong>musical</strong> - competencies in rhythm, pitch, tone, etc and ability to compose, play, and appreciate music
	- <strong>bodily-kinesthetic</strong> - the control and use of one's body
	- <strong>logical-mathematical</strong> - numerical and logical thinking
	- <strong>verbal-linguistic</strong> - the use of language and communication
	- <strong>visuospatial</strong> - the mental visual perceptions and manipulations
	- <strong>interpersonal</strong> - the discerning of others: emotional, motivations, etc
	- <strong>intrapersonal</strong> - knowing oneself: emotional, motivations, etc
	- <strong>naturalist</strong> - discerning patterns in nature
- additional tentative intelligence
	- <strong>spiritual</strong> - recognition of the spiritual
	- <strong>existential</strong> - concern with the ultimate state of being
	- <strong>pedagogical</strong> - teach smart \~2016

### Robert Sternberg’s Triarchic Theory of Intelligence
- divides intelligence into three dimensions that work together:
	- <strong>componential dimension</strong> - includes an individual's mental mechanisms:
		- meta-components - processes used in planning, monitoring, and evaluating the performance of a task. these direct all other mental activities
		- performance components - strategies for executing a task
		- knowledge acquisition components - processes involved in learning new things
	- <strong>experiential dimension</strong> - involves the way the individual deals with the internal and/or external world
	- <strong>contextual dimension</strong> - how individuals adapt to, shape, and select the external world around them

# Types of Intelligence
- <strong>fluid intelligence</strong> - indexed by tests of abstract reasoning and pattern detection
- <strong>crystallized intelligence</strong> - indexed by measures of vocabulary and general knowledge
- <strong>[[Ackerman's PPIK (Process - Personality - Intelligence - Knowledge) Theory|dark matter intelligence]] - </strong>domain-specific knowledge

# Impacts on Intelligence
### Genetic
- up to 80% of the variation found in adult human intelligence is thought to be attributable to genetics, despite the fact that it is a complicated, polygenic trait - controlled by the interaction of more than one gene

### Environmental
- environmental factors generally fall into 2 main categories:
	- <strong>biological influences</strong> - act on the physical body
	- <strong>sociocultural influences</strong> - act on mind and behavior

# Intelligence Amplification/Enhancement
- <strong>deep thinking</strong> - life is not just about reacting to events, rather we should have a conscious objective and select our actions to get nearer to our objective
- <strong>good reasoning</strong> - consciously performed in proper order:
	- have objective
	- make general sense of it
	- determine your decision based on your sensing
	- layout plan(s) that achieves the objective
	- select the best plan/results
	- implement your plan
	- observe results
	- store experiences for future references
- <strong>learning from past experience</strong>
	- it's believed that many inventions are actually re-inventions (exp. Egypt)
	- application: journal/writing
- <strong>experience and practice</strong>
	- you cannot learn to swim from a book
	- practice makes perfect
- <strong>the pygmalion effect</strong> - is a studied phenomenon in which higher expectations lead to better performance
- <strong>the golem effect</strong> - is the opposite phenomenon, in which lower expectations lead to lower performance
