###### Energy
````excerpt
- energy is the quantitativeproperty that must be transferred to an object in order to perform work on or to heat the object
- energy is a conserved quantity; the law of conservation of energy states that energy can be converted in form, but not created or destroyed
- [[Radiation|radiation]] is the transmission of energy
````
^excerpt

# Energy - Measurement

The [[Metric System - International System of Units (SI Units)|SI unit]] of energy is the [joule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joule):
- joule is the energy transferred to an object by the work of moving it a distance of 1 meter against a [[Force|force]] of 1 [newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newton_(unit)).
- joule = kg m<sup>2</sup> s<sup>−2</sup>

# Energy - Forms
common forms of energy include:
- [kinetic energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_energy) of a moving object
- [potential energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential_energy) stored by an object's position in a force [field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_field_theory) ([gravitational](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravitational_field), [electric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_field), or [magnetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetic_field))
- [elastic energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elastic_energy) stored by stretching solid objects
- [chemical energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemical_energy) released when fuel [burns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combustion)
- [radiant energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiant_energy) carried by light
- [thermal energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_energy) due to an object's [temperature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperature)
- etc

# Energy - Mass Relation

[Mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass) and energy are closely related. Due to [mass–energy equivalence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass%E2%80%93energy_equivalence), any object that has mass when stationary (called [rest mass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rest_mass)) also has an equivalent amount of energy whose form is called [rest energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rest_mass#Rest_energy), and any additional energy (of any form) acquired by the object above that resting energy will increase the object's total mass just as it increases its total energy.
