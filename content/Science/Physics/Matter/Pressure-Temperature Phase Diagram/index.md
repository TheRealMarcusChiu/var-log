# <strong>Points</strong>

###### Triple Point

the temperature and pressure at which the solid, liquid, and vapor phases of a pure substance can coexist in equilibrium.

###### Critical Point

the endpoint of a phase equilibrium curve. The most prominent example is the liquid-vapor critical point, the end point of the pressure-temperature curve that designates conditions under which a [liquid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid) and its [vapor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vapor) can coexist. At higher temperatures, the gas cannot be liquefied by pressure alone. At the critical point, defined by a <em>critical temperature</em><em>T</em><sub>c</sub> and a <em>critical pressure</em><em>p</em><sub>c</sub>, [phase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_\(matter\)) boundaries vanish
